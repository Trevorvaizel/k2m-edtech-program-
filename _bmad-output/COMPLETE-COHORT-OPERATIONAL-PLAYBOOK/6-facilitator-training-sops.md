# 6. FACILITATOR TRAINING & SOPs

## FACILITATOR SELECTION

**Who Makes a Good Facilitator:**
- Calm presence (not performers)
- Comfortable with NOT answering questions
- Can follow a script without improvising
- 15-20 min/day availability for Discord
- Reliable internet connection

**Who Does NOT:**
- People who love showing off what they know
- People who can't resist "fixing" student outputs
- People who need to be the smartest person in the room

## 2-WEEK FACILITATOR TRAINING

**Week 1: Experience the Cohort**
- [ ] Go through the 6-week experience as a participant
- [ ] Practice NOT answering
- [ ] Learn intervention rules
- [ ] Read this entire playbook

**Week 2: Shadow + Practice**
- [ ] Shadow 2 live sessions
- [ ] Run 1 session with supervision
- [ ] Debrief language use
- [ ] Get signed off as ready

## FACILITATOR INTERVENTION RULES

**When TO Engage:**
- Technical confusion (how to use Discord)
- Emotional distress
- Someone hasn't posted in 3+ days

**When NOT TO Engage:**
- "Is my prompt good?" (Let them experience)
- "Can someone help me improve this?" (Not allowed)
- Tool questions during practice
- Debates about approaches

**What TO Say:**
- "Notice what happened there."
- "That reaction makes sense."
- "What do you think changed?"
- "You don't need to understand this fully yet."

**What NOT TO Say:**
- "The right way to do this is..."
- "Here's a better prompt..."
- "You should try..."
- "Actually, it works better if..."
