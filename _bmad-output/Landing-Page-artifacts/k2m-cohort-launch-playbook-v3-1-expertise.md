# MODEL 6 (V3.1): ANAGNORISIS — EXPERTISE EDITION

## The Recognition Is Different

In the Intelligence Pillar, the recognition is: *"I'm in-between and that's okay — there's a way to use this time."*

In the Expertise Pillar, the recognition is: *"Access was unfair — and now it doesn't have to be."*

```
EXPERTISE ANAGNORISIS STRUCTURE:

BEAT 1 — THE MIRROR (Access Gap)
  Show the structural reality she already secretly knows.
  Not named as inequality — just named as how things work.
  "Getting good at economics used to mean taking
   a course, finding a tutor, or knowing someone in finance."
  She thinks: "That's true. That's how it works."

BEAT 2 — THE SHIFT (Fairness Revealed)
  One thing has changed. Just one.
  "It no longer does."
  OR: "You don't need to have studied it.
       You need to know how to access it."
  She thinks: "Wait. That's different. Is that true?"

BEAT 3 — THE DOOR (Proof of the Shift)
  The door is also the proof. K2M is the evidence
  that the shift is real and accessible.
  "15 spots. Reply THINK."

KEY DIFFERENCE FROM INTELLIGENCE PILLAR:
  Intelligence: recognition is about HER inner state
  Expertise:    recognition is about the WORLD'S changed structure
  Both use anagnorisis — the trigger is just different.
```

# MODEL 10 (V3.1): CONCRETIZATION ENGINE — EXPERTISE EDITION

## The Problem With Prompt Demos for Expertise

The Intelligence Pillar's concretization shows a better prompt producing a better AI response. It demonstrates skill. But for the expertise story, the demo is more powerful when it shows **what she was able to access** — not HOW she accessed it.

```
EXPERTISE CONCRETIZATION:
  Don't show the tool.
  Show what became possible.
  AI is backstage. Her capability is the story.
```

## The Expertise Demo Pattern

```
STEP 1 — SETUP (5 seconds)
  "Let me show you something."
  (One sentence. Creates expectation.)

STEP 2 — THE BEFORE (15 seconds)
  A Form 4 graduate encounters a domain she's never studied.
  She searches. She gets surface information.
  General. Shallow. Could be anyone's answer.
  "She doesn't know what she doesn't know."

STEP 3 — THE AFTER (15 seconds)
  Same student. 8 weeks of K2M.
  Same domain. Different approach.
  She doesn't just search — she structures her thinking.
  She asks the right questions. She frames the right context.
  The result: she produces something that shows she understands it.
  Not copied. Not summarized. Understood and applied.

STEP 4 — THE QUESTION (10 seconds)
  "What's different?"
  (Let them answer. The silence is productive.)

STEP 5 — THE NAME (5 seconds)
  "She didn't study economics. She learned how to access it."
  (Now "democratized expertise" has a felt referent.)

STEP 6 — THE DOOR (10 seconds)
  "That's what K2M builds. 8 weeks. 15 spots.
   Reply THINK if you want details."
```
