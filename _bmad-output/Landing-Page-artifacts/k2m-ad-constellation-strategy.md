## THE TERRITORY: WHO WE ARE TALKING TO

**Not school students. Not university students.**

> **Form 4 graduates in the gap period — waiting to begin university.**

### The Specific Portrait
- 17–19 years old, East African (Nairobi and surrounding context)
- Exams are done. Placement is pending or confirmed.
- The group chat is moving at different speeds. Some friends got jobs. Some went straight to work.
- She uses ChatGPT. Gets results. But doesn't know if it's hers.
- She doesn't feel idle — she feels **in-between.** Busy but directionless.
- The question underneath everything: *"Will I be someone who belongs in the future that's coming?"*

### The Three Real Jobs (JTBD — grounded in territory)

| Job Type | What She's Actually Hiring For |
|---|---|
| **Functional** | *"Give me something real to do in this gap that isn't just waiting."* |
| **Emotional** | *"Tell me I won't be behind when I get there."* |
| **Social** | *"Give me something I can say I did — a story I can tell at orientation."* |

### The Emotional Arc We're Entering
```
Anxiety → Curiosity → Confidence → Ownership → Authorship
```
Our ads meet her at **Anxiety.** K2M walks her to **Authorship.**

## THE ANAGNORISIS PRINCIPLE

Every ad in this constellation is built on **anagnorisis** — the Greek dramatic term for the moment of sudden recognition. The gasp. The "that's me." The instant everything clicks.

**Structure of every node-ad (three beats):**
```
BEAT 1 — THE MIRROR     (5 seconds)
Show the node so specifically the viewer thinks "how do they know?"
Not inspired. Just SEEN.

BEAT 2 — THE SHIFT      (20–40 seconds)
One thing changes. Not a montage. ONE moment.
The pause. The question asked differently. A face changing.

BEAT 3 — THE DOOR       (5 seconds)
Not a CTA. An opening.
"This is where it started for her."
"6 weeks. K2M."
```

## PRE-MORTEM: ALL NODES
*"It's 3 months later. Each ad dropped. Each one failed. Why?"*

---

### NODE 1 — THE RESULT LETTER

**Failure modes:**
- Opens on paralysis — misses students still in the relief/celebration phase
- For those who underperformed, the letter image presses a wound — they click away before beat 2
- Timing mismatch: too early for some, too late for others

**What success requires:**
```
→ Don't show the letter. Show what happens AFTER the letter settles.
→ Target to run 3–4 weeks post-results, not immediately after
→ The emotion: not paralysis — the quiet that comes after the noise.
   "The exams are done. The waiting has started."
   This lands for everyone, regardless of grade.
```

---

### NODE 2 — THE GROUP CHAT

**Failure modes:**
- WhatsApp simulation looks fake — intimacy is precisely what makes it unfilmable
- Amplifies anxiety without resolving it within 30 seconds — viewer left MORE anxious
- The trigger is bigger than the relief

**What success requires:**
```
→ Never show the chat. Show the FACE of someone reading it.
   One expression. No text needed. Everyone knows that face.
→ The shift: she puts the phone down and opens something else. Intentionally.
→ The ad shouldn't resolve the anxiety — it should REDIRECT it.
   "That energy? Point it here."
```

---

### NODE 3 — THE PARENT QUESTION

**Failure modes:**
- Parent-child tension framing makes the parent the antagonist — rejected by both audiences
- Student feels exposed. Parent feels accused.
- The story we want to tell requires the parent to be the WITNESS, not the pressure

**What success requires:**
```
→ Reframe: not "parent interrogates daughter"
   but "parent doesn't know how to help, then witnesses the shift"
→ The parent is not the pressure — the parent is the witness.
→ This ad is FOR parents to share to students.
   Flip the audience. The daughter is the hero. The parent is moved.
   "She didn't know how to help. Then she saw what K2M gave her."
```

---

### NODE 4 — THE UNIVERSITY THRESHOLD

**Failure modes:**
- Veo cannot render authentic Kenyan university settings — defaults to Western aesthetics, breaks the illusion
- Sells fear relief for a threat that's months away — urgency mismatch kills conversion
- The future arrival isn't urgent enough to convert today

**What success requires:**
```
→ Don't show the university. Show the FEELING of threshold —
   a doorway, a first step, a new room. Universal images of beginning.
→ Shift the urgency: not "you'll be ready when you arrive"
   but "the person who arrives is being built RIGHT NOW."
   Make the gap the urgent moment, not the future arrival.
→ Veo prompt: a doorway she's never walked through.
   Morning light. Her hand on the door. Before she pushes it open.
```

---

### NODE 5 — THE AI CONFUSION

**Failure modes:**
- She doesn't think she's confused — she uses ChatGPT daily and feels functional
- "You're doing it wrong" framing triggers defensiveness — nobody shares an ad that made them feel stupid
- Split-screen prompt comparison looks like a tutorial — tutorials don't go viral

**What success requires:**
```
→ Never suggest she's doing it wrong.
   Suggest there's a version of this she hasn't felt yet.
   "You've used AI. But have you ever had it feel like YOUR thinking?"
→ The anagnorisis: not "I was wrong" — "I didn't know this was possible."
   Surprise, not correction.
→ Show the EXPRESSION, not the screen.
   Her face when the response feels like hers.
   That's the 3 seconds the entire ad builds to.
```

---

### NODE 6 — THE IDLE AFTERNOON

**Failure modes:**
- "Idle" is a middle-class problem — many graduates are working, helping at home, caring for siblings
- The cinematic still-life aesthetic aestheticizes aimlessness — feels like poverty tourism
- The golden-hour girl is invisible to the student who has too much to do and still feels lost

**What success requires:**
```
→ Replace "idle" with "in-between."
   She's not doing nothing — she's doing something that doesn't feel like enough.
   Helping in the shop. Scrolling. Waiting for a matatu. Busy but directionless.
→ Visual: textured, real, warm — not cinematic still life.
   Movement in the background. Life happening around her.
   SHE is the still point in a moving world.
→ Veo prompt: not "girl sitting alone" but "girl moving through
   a busy everyday scene, slightly out of sync with the pace around her."
```

---

### NODE 7 — THE COMPARISON SPIRAL ❌ RETIRED

**Why it's cut:**
- Directly violates K2M brand guardrails: Revolutionary Hope, not anxiety
- Fear-based conversion attracts the wrong audience — highest churn risk
- Amplifies a toxic emotion without the program to resolve it in 30 seconds

**How it lives instead:**
```
→ The Comparison Spiral is the WATER the protagonist swims in.
   It's context, not content.
→ If referenced at all: "Not a race. A different track."
   One line. Immediate pivot to her own journey. Never linger.
```

---

### NODE 8 — THE IDENTITY QUESTION

**Failure modes:**
- Level 4–5 philosophy served to a Level 1–2 cold audience — she feels spoken AT, not seen
- "Who am I becoming?" is the question she's asking internally but won't walk through voluntarily
- Showing the summit before the trailhead

**What success requires:**
```
→ This node doesn't become a standalone ad.
   It becomes the PAYOFF LINE of every other ad.
→ Last 5 seconds of Node 5, Node 6, Node 4 — they all end here.
   A whisper, not a headline.
   "She found out who she was becoming."
→ This IS the through-line of the entire constellation.
   The hidden spine. Never the opening. Always the close.
```

---

### NODE 9 — THE FIRST TIME IT WORKED

**Failure modes:**
- The ad only creates anagnorisis in someone who has ALREADY had this experience
- Cold audiences can't recognize a moment they haven't lived
- Micro-expression authenticity cannot be generated by Veo at this fidelity

**What success requires:**
```
→ Use a REAL face. This is the one ad where Veo isn't enough.
   15 seconds. One person. One genuine moment.
→ Reframe for cold audiences: not "recognize this moment"
   but "imagine the first time AI said something
   that felt like it came from YOU."
   Invite imagination, don't demand recognition.
→ This is the RETARGETING ad — shown after one of the first-touch ads.
   Second touch, not first.
```

---

### NODE 10 — THE ARTIFACT MOMENT

**Failure modes:**
- Wrong funnel stage — graduation ad shown to unenrolled viewers breaks emotional logic
- Who witnesses her presentation? Every version of the witness has a problem.
- Cold audiences can't aspire to a moment they don't have the context to imagine

**What success requires:**
```
→ The artifact moment is ORGANIC social proof, not produced ad.
   It's what graduates post themselves.
→ If it becomes an ad: the witness is HER OWN PAST SELF.
   She looks at something she made and can barely believe it's hers.
   No audience needed. Just her and the proof.
→ Final ad in the sequence — shown to warm leads only.
   The closing argument, not the opening statement.
```

## PRODUCTION SEQUENCE

**Rule: Don't make all nine at once.**

### Phase 1 — Build & Test (First-Touch Constellation)
Make these three first. Test which one finds its people fastest.

| Priority | Node | Why First |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Node 6 — "In-Between" | Most universal. Hits regardless of grade, class, schedule. |
| 2 | Node 2 — "The Face" | Shortest. Cheapest. Most shareable. Tests the emotional hook. |
| 3 | Node 5 — "Not Yet" | Most differentiated. Tests whether "surprise not correction" lands. |

### Phase 2 — Expand (Second-Touch Constellation)
Build these based on which Phase 1 ad performed. Match follow-up tone to winner.

### Phase 3 — Close (Third-Touch Constellation)
Shown only to warm leads who've already engaged. The conversion layer.

## CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE

*The function of the ad is to convert. Beautiful emotion without conversion path is a donation to the algorithm.*

### The Fogg Equation — Applied
```
B = MOTIVATION × ABILITY × PROMPT

MOTIVATION  → Built by Zone 4 cinematography and anagnorisis
ABILITY     → "DM THINK" — one word, WhatsApp, zero friction
PROMPT      → Delivered AT the anagnorisis — not after
```

### The Conversion Reality (Gen Z Nairobi)
| What She Has | What She Will Do |
|---|---|
| Tecno / Samsung A-series | Watch on TikTok / YouTube |
| WhatsApp always open | DM if friction is zero |
| Precious data bundles | Will NOT visit a slow website |
| No credit card | Mpesa or nothing |
| Trust through proximity | Trusts people, not forms |
| Cohort = social proof | "Who else is joining?" |

### The Micro-Commitment Ladder
```
TIER 1 — COLD (First touch)
"DM THINK" → WhatsApp number
One word. The word itself IS the transformation.
Sending it is the first act of thinking with intention.

TIER 2 — WARM (Second touch)
"Cohort 3 — 15 spots" + landing page link
Scarcity is real, specific, hope-based not fear-based.

TIER 3 — HOT (Third touch)
"Secure your spot — Cohort 3 opens [date]"
Direct application. Mpesa + form. Now she's ready.
```


## ZONE 4 PROMPT PRINCIPLES
*What separates evocative prompts from descriptions*

```
01. PROMPT THE BODY, NOT THE FACE
    The face follows the body. The body never lies.
    "Her shoulders are 3 degrees forward" >
    "she looks sad"

02. ONE SPECIFIC OBJECT UNLOCKS THE WORLD
    One crocheted doily > "a modest Kenyan home"
    One ka-sufuria > "East African kitchen"
    The specific IS the universal.

03. LIGHT IS PSYCHOLOGY, NOT DECORATION
    Name quality, direction, temperature precisely.
    "4:45pm Nairobi white-gold from the left" >
    "afternoon light"

04. GIVE THE CAMERA A POSTURE
    "The camera holds the way a good witness holds —
    without blinking, without explaining."
    Veo responds to the camera's RELATIONSHIP to subject.

05. PROMPT THE GAP BETWEEN WHAT WE SEE AND WHAT WE KNOW
    "The room breathes without her."
    "She is between two illuminations."
    The gap is where evocation lives.

06. NAME THE CINEMATOGRAPHER REFERENCE — PRECISELY
    Bradford Young: warm, documentary, respectful distance
    Roger Deakins: cold precision, geometric framing
    Emmanuel Lubezki: continuous, immersive, nature-lit
    Veo has ingested these. Use the language.

07. HOLD LONGER THAN COMFORTABLE
    "Hold 5 seconds. Do not cut early."
    Evocation requires time.
    The discomfort of holding IS the feeling.

08. LENS = EMOTIONAL DISTANCE
    35mm → Environmental. Person inside a world.
    50mm → Human. You are standing in the room with her.
    85mm → Intimate. Only her. Only this moment.
    For anagnorisis: always 85mm.
```

### THE DIRECTOR'S FORMULA (applied to every shot)
```
1. BACKGROUND/SETTING  → Environment, location, time, weather, specific objects
2. SUBJECT             → Exact physical description. "A withered sailor" not "a man"
3. ACTION              → Physics-based movement. What the body actually does
4. CAMERA              → Angle + shot type + motion (low angle tracking, dolly zoom, crane)
5. LENS                → Focal length + format (35mm anamorphic, 85mm spherical, 8mm wide)
6. LIGHTING            → Named, directional, motivated (golden hour, Rembrandt, cinematic noir)
7. STYLE + TECHNICAL   → Cinematic reference + 4K + fps + filter
8. SOUND               → Dialogue (colon, not quotes) + SFX layers (FG/MG/BG) + music
                         (define: instruments + tempo + mood)
```

**SHOT 01 — 0:00–0:04 | THE UNIFORM**

```
BACKGROUND/SETTING:
Interior of a Nairobi estate flat sitting room — Rongai or Ruaka.
Concrete walls painted faded cream, chipping at the skirting board.
A china cabinet with never-opened wedding crockery behind glass.
School photographs in plastic clip frames asymmetric on the wall.
A 2024 calendar two months behind. A Family Bible beside a Nokia
charging cable on a CRT television stand. A sofa with a crocheted
antimacassar draped over the right armrest. Late morning — overcast
equatorial sky diffusing flat white light through a polyester curtain
with a geometric rust-and-cream pattern, cut slightly too short,
hanging 4 inches above the window sill.

SUBJECT:
No person. A KCSE-era white school blouse and dark pleated skirt,
folded with ceremonious precision — collar corners aligned, pleat
stacked — resting on a faded teal plastic chair. The KCSE results
certificate is partially visible beneath it, the Kenya National
Examinations Council logo and red border legible at the corner.

ACTION:
Dust motes drift slowly through a thin shaft of light piercing
the curtain seam. The curtain lifts once — barely 2cm — in a draft
from beneath the door. Then settles. The room is completely still.

CAMERA:
Static locked-off. Eye level, frame positioned just below the chair
seat so the folded uniform occupies the upper-centre third of frame.
No motion. The camera breathes once — barely.

LENS:
50mm anamorphic 2.39:1. Slight widescreen cinematic format.
Natural oval bokeh in the background — the china cabinet and
calendar soft but readable. Foreground: the uniform in sharp focus.

LIGHTING:
Diffused overcast exterior light through the curtain — flat, milky,
slightly overexposed on the white blouse. No shadows. No drama.
The uniform receives the most light in the frame. The overhead
fluorescent tube: off. No artificial light. Practical ambient only.

STYLE + TECHNICAL:
4K, 24fps. ARRI Alexa Mini LF color science reference.
Warm highlights. Slightly lifted blacks — the shadows are gentle.
1/4 black mist filter — the overexposed white blouse blooms softly
at the edges rather than clipping. Cinematic realism.
The image should feel like the last page of a chapter.

SOUND:
ROOM TONE: the low refrigerator hum from the kitchen — 60Hz,
  constant, -40dB. The electrical presence of an occupied space.
SFX LAYER 1 (BG): through the concrete wall — a neighbor's radio,
  Swahili gospel music, the timbre of KBC Radio, barely intelligible,
  -35dB. A presence more than a sound.
SFX LAYER 2 (BG): a matatu horn bleating once, two streets away.
  Then: the bass of its engine fading. Then: silence.
SFX LAYER 3 (FG): the curtain fabric lifting and settling —
  a soft polyester whisper, 0.5 seconds. Then nothing.
MUSIC: silence. No music. The room holds its breath.
```

**SHOT 02 — 0:04–0:12 | THE IN-BETWEEN BODY**

```
BACKGROUND/SETTING:
The same estate flat — small kitchen opening off the sitting room.
A two-burner LPG gas cooker on a concrete countertop painted with
old oil stains. A ka-sufuria with dried tea residue sits on the
left burner, unused. A plastic dish rack holds three mismatched cups.
A soap bar worn to a flat sliver rests on the sink edge.
A small louvred window above the sink: through the frosted glass,
the silhouette of a concrete perimeter wall, bougainvillea pushing
pink over the top. Flat equatorial midday sky above — white-bright,
no cloud. Laundry hanging on a line is faintly visible through
a half-open door to the left.

SUBJECT:
Zawadi. 18 years old. Oval face with high natural cheekbones,
full lips, no makeup. Natural 4C hair loosely pulled back with a
fabric scrunchie — loose strands escaping at the temples and nape.
Oversized grey hoodie washed to near-white softness, a university
name barely legible on the chest. Black leggings. Pink rubber
flip-flops. Small gold ball studs, both ears. A thin horizontal
scar on her left forearm — barely visible, from childhood.

ACTION:
Her left hand holds a single ceramic cup. Her right hand draws
a pink soap bar across the cup exterior — suds form, thick and white.
She rinses under a low-pressure tap: the water comes out aerated,
not a clean stream. She places the cup upside-down on the plastic
rack — a precise, automatic motion. 8 seconds total. Zero variation
from a thousand previous repetitions. Her head does not track
the task at any point. Her gaze is fixed beyond the louvred window —
not at the bougainvillea. Beyond it. At something that isn't there.
Her shoulders sit 3 degrees forward — the posture of someone
who has been indoors too long. Her jaw hangs loose.

CAMERA:
Handheld medium shot — from her waist to 2 inches above her crown.
Positioned 2 feet behind her left shoulder, at a 45-degree angle —
her profile and the window visible simultaneously.
Camera breathes with the operator: a slow, organic vertical drift
of 3cm over 8 seconds. No stabilisation correction applied.
Documentary witness position — present, non-intrusive.

LENS:
35mm spherical. Slight environmental width — the kitchen walls
press in around her. The window is in focus behind her.

LIGHTING:
Hard midday exterior light through the louvred window: overexposed
on the concrete wall beyond, creating a bright backlight halo
around the edges of her loose hair. Her face: three-quarter shadow,
turned away from camera. No fill light — the interior is darker
than the exterior by 3 stops. High contrast. Motivated entirely
by the window. No artificial lights.

STYLE + TECHNICAL:
4K, 24fps. Bradford Young color reference — warm skin tones,
underexposed interior shadows that hold detail without crushing,
analog highlight rolloff at the window. No LUTs until grade.

SOUND:
SFX LAYER 1 (FG): water running from a low-pressure tap —
  aerated, slightly turbulent, not a clean stream. The specific
  sound of 1.5 bar domestic water pressure, 2 seconds.
SFX LAYER 2 (FG): the ceramic cup against the metal sink rim
  as she repositions — a soft hollow ring, 0.3 seconds.
SFX LAYER 3 (FG): rubber flip-flop on concrete as she shifts
  her weight left — a quiet rubber squeak, 0.2 seconds.
SFX LAYER 4 (MG): from the sitting room — a Swahili radio news
  presenter, KBC Radio timbre, gender-neutral voice, barely
  intelligible. A continuous gentle presence.
SFX LAYER 5 (BG): from outside the window — a boda boda engine
  decelerating from 40km/h to idle at the stage below, then:
  a matatu tout calling: Rongai! Rongai! — a practiced shout,
  fading as the matatu pulls away. Then: quiet.
MUSIC: the first sound enters. A single piano key — middle C —
  played with a light touch on an upright piano, lightly dampened.
  The note is held 2 seconds. Then decays. Then: only its reverb
  remaining for 1 second. Then: silence.
```
