#### DAY 4 (Thursday): Student Story Test (X Thread)

**Content Type:** Masterpiece (Territory-First)
**Platform:** X Thread
**Time:** 2 hours

```
I spent 3 months feeling like I was cheating every time I used ChatGPT.

I'd paste a prompt, get an answer, and think "this isn't real work."

So I wouldn't tell anyone.

I'd do the AI work in secret, then hand-write parts of my assignments so it looked "real."

I thought I was the only one.

Then I talked to 20 students.

19 of them said the same thing.

← The thread

Here's what I realized:

The anxiety wasn't about AI. It was about how I was thinking about work itself.

I was trying to delegate decisions I hadn't even defined.

So I started doing this one thing before every AI session:

I'd write down what I was actually trying to solve. Not what I wanted AI to produce—what I was trying to understand.

That tiny shift—defining before delegating—changed everything:

• Suddenly, AI outputs were actually useful
• I was learning MORE, not less
• I stopped feeling like I was cheating

Here's the full framework of 4 habits that took me from "AI feels like cheating" to "I think WITH AI" →

HABIT 1: Define Before You Delegate
Before opening AI, write down:
• What problem am I solving?
• What am I trying to understand?
• What do I already know?

HABIT 2: Iterate, Don't Order
Don't prompt once and accept. Have a conversation:
• "That's good, but can you explain X?"
• "What if we tried Y instead?"
• "Help me understand why you chose Z"

HABIT 3: Inspect to Calibrate
Never accept AI output at face value:
• Does this match what I know?
• What feels off here?
• Where do I need to add my own thinking?

HABIT 4: Reflect to Compound
After each AI session:
• What worked? What didn't?
• What would I do differently?
• What did I learn about how I think?

These 4 habits changed everything.

I went from "cheating" to "thinking WITH AI" in 6 weeks.

The students who learn these habits?
They're not behind. They're amplified.

Want to build these habits before university?
Link in bio for the full journey.

#AI #Students #Learning #FutureSkills
```

**Metric to Track:** Thread reads + Link clicks
