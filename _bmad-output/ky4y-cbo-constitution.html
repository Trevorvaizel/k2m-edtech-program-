KY4Y - ConstitutionPreamble

Preamble

We, the founding members of the KASIPUL YOUTH FOR YOUTH MOVEMENT, coming together as youth of Kasipul Constituency, do hereby establish this organization in the conviction that:

  • The youth of Kasipul are not a problem to be managed — they are a majority to be mobilized
  • Lasting empowerment comes not from what is given, but from what is built together
  • Peer-to-peer support — youth serving youth — is the most sustainable engine of community transformation
  • Transparent, accountable institutions outlast any individual leader

We adopt this Constitution as the foundation of our community, our commitment to one another, and our covenant with Kasipul.

Article 1: Name and Registered Office

1.1 The name of this organization shall be the KASIPUL YOUTH FOR YOUTH MOVEMENT, hereinafter referred to as "KY4Y" or "the Movement."
1.2 The registered physical address of KY4Y shall be within Kasipul Constituency, Rachuonyo South Sub-County, Homa Bay County, Kenya.
1.3 KY4Y shall maintain an official email address, postal address, and telephone number, details of which shall be filed with the Registering Authority and updated within 30 days of any change.

Article 2: Interpretation

In this Constitution, unless the context otherwise requires:

"Act" means the Community Groups Registration Act No. 30 of 2022 and any amendments thereto.
"AGM" means the Annual General Meeting of the Movement.
"Chairperson" means the elected head of the Executive Committee.
"Director of the Department" means the Director of the Department of Gender and Social Development under the Ministry of Labour and Social Protection.
"Honorary Patron" means the serving Member of Parliament for Kasipul Constituency, holding a non-executive, non-voting ceremonial and advocacy role within KY4Y as provided under Article 14.
"Executive Committee" means the governing body of KY4Y as constituted under Article 6.
"Financial Year" means the period from 1st January to 31st December of each calendar year.
"General Assembly" means the supreme decision-making body comprising all paid-up members in good standing.
"Member" means any person duly admitted to KY4Y in accordance with this Constitution.

Article 3: Objectives and Purposes

3.1 Primary Objectives

KY4Y is established to pursue the following objectives within Kasipul Constituency:

(a) To empower youth aged 18–35 economically, socially, and intellectually through peer-to-peer mentorship, skills development, and enterprise incubation.
(b) To facilitate the creation and growth of youth-owned enterprises, cooperatives, and income-generating projects within Kasipul Constituency.
(c) To build a culture of excellence, accountability, and mutual support among the youth of Kasipul — grounded in the principle that youth help youth.
(d) To advocate for the interests, rights, and opportunities of young people in Kasipul Constituency before government institutions, private sector entities, and civil society.
(e) To connect Kasipul youth to regional, national, and global networks, resources, and opportunities in the digital economy, skilled trades, and entrepreneurship.
(f) To document and share the "Kasipul Model" of youth-led development as a replicable framework for other communities in Kenya.
(g) To promote civic engagement, transparency, and accountable leadership among youth in Kasipul Constituency.

3.2 Ancillary Activities

In furtherance of its objectives, KY4Y may:

  • Organize training programs, workshops, and mentorship sessions
  • Partner with government agencies, private sector organizations, NGOs, and international bodies
  • Receive grants, donations, and contributions from lawful sources
  • Establish Ward-level chapters and peer networks across all 5 wards of Kasipul Constituency
  • Publish reports, newsletters, and communications documenting its activities and impact
  • Manage projects and programs consistent with its objectives

3.3 Non-Political Clause

KY4Y is non-partisan and shall not engage in partisan political activities.

Article 4: Membership

4.1 Nature of Membership

Membership of KY4Y is open to all eligible persons who meet the criteria set out in Article 4.2 and subscribe to the values and objectives of the Movement.

4.2 Eligibility for Membership

Any person is eligible for membership in KY4Y if they:

(a) Are a Kenyan citizen or legal resident aged 18 to 35 years at the time of application;
(b) Reside, work, study, or have a demonstrated connection to Kasipul Constituency, Homa Bay County;
(c) Agree to abide by this Constitution, the Movement's values, and any rules or policies adopted by the General Assembly;
(d) Pay the prescribed membership subscription fee as determined by the General Assembly from time to time.
Note: Associate membership (non-voting, advisory) may be extended to persons outside the 18–35 age range who wish to support KY4Y's mission, subject to Executive Committee approval. Associate members may not attend Executive Committee meetings, participate in ExCo deliberations, hold any advisory role in governance decisions, or represent KY4Y externally in any official capacity. Associate membership confers no governance rights of any kind.

4.3 Admission of Members

(a) A person wishing to join KY4Y shall complete a membership application form as prescribed by the Executive Committee.
(b) The application shall be submitted to the Secretary General together with the membership subscription fee.
(c) The Executive Committee shall review and approve or decline applications within 14 days of receipt. Reasons for any decline shall be communicated in writing.
(d) Upon approval, the applicant's name shall be entered into the Member Register and the applicant shall receive a membership card or acknowledgment.

4.4 Membership Subscription

(a) The annual membership subscription fee shall be determined by the General Assembly.
(b) The initial annual subscription fee is KSh 1000 per member, payable upon joining and annually thereafter.
(c) The subscription fee may be waived or subsidized for members demonstrating financial hardship, subject to Executive Committee approval.
(d) A member who fails to pay their annual subscription within 3 months of the due date shall be considered a member not in good standing and shall lose voting rights until the arrears are settled.

4.5 Rights of Members

Every member in good standing shall have the right to:

(a) Attend and participate in General Assembly meetings;

4.9 Removal of Members

(a) A member may be removed from KY4Y for: gross misconduct or behavior bringing KY4Y into disrepute; persistent non-payment of subscriptions (beyond 6 months in arrears); acting against the interests, values, or objectives of KY4Y; or conviction of a criminal offence.
(b) Before removal, the member shall be given written notice of the alleged conduct, afforded an opportunity to respond in writing or in person before the Executive Committee within 14 days, and informed of the decision in writing with reasons.
(c) A member aggrieved by a removal decision may appeal to the General Assembly. The decision of the General Assembly on appeal shall be final.
(d) A removed member's name shall be struck from the Member Register.

Article 5: Discipline

5.1 The Executive Committee shall be responsible for maintaining the discipline and conduct standards of KY4Y.
5.2 The following disciplinary measures may be applied in ascending order of severity: (a) Verbal Warning — for minor infractions; (b) Written Warning — for repeated minor infractions or first moderate infractions; (c) Suspension — temporary removal of membership rights for a specified period; (d) Removal — permanent removal from membership (per Article 4.9).
5.3 All disciplinary proceedings shall follow natural justice principles: the member shall be informed of the allegation and given a fair opportunity to respond before any sanction is applied.
5.4 A member shall have the right to appeal any disciplinary decision to the General Assembly within 21 days of receiving the decision. The General Assembly's ruling on appeal is final.

6.4 Election of Officials

A. Notice and Scheduling

(a) Elections shall be held at the AGM (in election years) or at a Special General Meeting convened specifically for that purpose.
(b) The Secretary General shall give all members at least 21 days' notice that elections will be held, specifying the positions to be contested, the date, time, and venue.
- 10 -
KY4Y - ConstitutionA. Notice and Scheduling (continued)
(c) Elections shall be presided over by a Presiding Officer — a neutral person appointed by the General Assembly from among members in good standing who is NOT contesting any position. The Presiding Officer must not have a known personal, financial, or political relationship with any candidate, as confirmed by the General Assembly before appointment. Any member may object to a proposed Presiding Officer on grounds of partiality; the General Assembly shall rule on the objection by simple majority before proceeding. The Presiding Officer's role is to ensure a fair, orderly process and to declare results. Scrutineers (minimum two) shall be nominated by candidates collectively, not appointed by the Presiding Officer.

B. Nominations

(d) Any eligible member wishing to contest a position shall submit a written Nomination Form to the Secretary General at least 7 days before the election date. The Nomination Form shall include: the candidate's full name, ID number, and membership tier; the position being contested; a declaration that the candidate meets all eligibility criteria under Article 6.2; a declaration of any conflict of interest; signatures of two (2) members in good standing who propose and second the nomination; and the candidate's signed acceptance.
(e) The Secretary General shall verify eligibility and publish the list of confirmed candidates at least 5 days before the election date.
(f) If no valid nomination is received for a position, the General Assembly may nominate a candidate from the floor on election day, subject to that candidate meeting all eligibility criteria and accepting the nomination.

C. Campaigning Rules

(g) Campaigning by candidates is permitted from the date nominations close until the day before the election. The following rules apply:
  • Candidates shall campaign only on the basis of their ideas, plans, and character
  • No candidate shall use KY4Y resources, funds, equipment, or platforms for personal campaigning
  • No candidate shall offer gifts, money, food, drinks, or any material benefit to members in exchange for votes (vote-buying) — any candidate found to have done so shall be immediately disqualified
  • No candidate shall make defamatory statements about other candidates
  • All candidates shall have equal opportunity to address members at a candidates' forum, organized by the Presiding Officer, not less than 3 days before election day
(h) Any complaint about campaigning conduct shall be submitted in writing to the Presiding Officer, who shall investigate and rule within 48 hours.

D. Voting

(i) All members in good standing present at the election meeting are entitled to vote.

KY4Y - ConstitutionD. Voting (continued)
(j) Elections shall be conducted by secret ballot where there is more than one candidate. Where only one candidate has been nominated and confirmed eligible, that candidate shall be declared elected by acclamation.
(k) The Presiding Officer shall organize, supervise, and count the ballot. At least two scrutineers (nominated by candidates collectively) shall observe and verify the count.
(l) A candidate receiving the simple majority of valid votes cast shall be declared elected.
(m) In the event of a tie, a re-vote shall be held immediately. If the tie persists, the matter shall be resolved by drawing of lots conducted by the Presiding Officer.
(n) Results shall be announced by the Presiding Officer immediately upon conclusion of counting for each position.

E. Taking Office

(o) Elected officials shall take office by making the following Pledge of Office before the General Assembly:
"I, [full name], having been elected to the position of [position] of the Kasipul Youth for Youth Movement, do solemnly pledge to serve the Movement and its members faithfully and honestly; to uphold this Constitution and the laws of Kenya; to act in the interests of the youth of Kasipul and not for personal gain; and to surrender this office peacefully at the end of my term or upon the lawful direction of the General Assembly. So help me."
(p) An outgoing official shall continue in office and hand over all records, accounts, assets, and passwords to their successor within 7 days of the election results being declared.
(q) No official shall retain any KY4Y property, documents, accounts access, or digital credentials beyond 7 days of leaving office.

6.5 Transitional Provisions (Founding Period)

(a) The founding Executive Committee shall serve a transitional term not exceeding 12 months from the date of CBO registration, after which a full election open to all eligible members shall be held.
(b) The first Annual General Meeting shall be held not later than 6 months from the date of CBO registration. Elections shall be held at this meeting or at a Special General Meeting called within the 12-month window.
(c) For the purpose of the founding election only, the Mentor tier requirement for the Chairperson position under Article 6.2 is waived. From the second election onwards, the Mentor tier requirement applies in full.
(d) Founding members who are appointed to Executive Committee positions under Article 15 shall be subject to all rules, responsibilities, and powers of this Constitution from the date of registration, and shall stand for election at the first General Meeting.

6.7 Vacancy in Office

(a) An office becomes vacant upon: resignation (written notice to the Secretary General); removal following disciplinary proceedings; death; failure to attend 3 consecutive Executive Committee meetings without reasonable cause; or ceasing to be a member in good standing.
(b) Where a vacancy arises, the Executive Committee may appoint an acting replacement from among members in good standing until a by-election is held within 60 days.
(c) Where the Chairperson's position falls vacant, the remaining Executive Committee members shall appoint an Acting Chairperson from among themselves by majority vote until a by-election is held within 60 days.

6.8 Ward Representatives

(a) Each of the 5 wards of Kasipul Constituency shall have one Ward Representative elected by KY4Y members resident in that ward.
(b) Ward Representatives shall: coordinate KY4Y activities within their ward; serve as the primary point of contact between ward members and the Executive Committee; report ward-level activities and needs to the Executive Committee quarterly; and attend Executive Committee meetings as non-voting participants (unless also holding an Executive Committee position).
(c) Ward Representatives shall serve a term of 2 years, renewable once.
(d) Ward Representative elections shall follow the same nomination, notice, campaigning, voting, and handover procedures as Executive Committee elections under Article 6.4, presided over by the Secretary General or their designee. A Ward Representative election shall be conducted separately for each ward, with only KY4Y members resident in that ward eligible to vote in their respective ward election.

6.9 Conflict of Interest Register

(a) The Secretary General shall maintain a Conflict of Interest Register as a permanent record of KY4Y.
(b) All Executive Committee officials shall declare in writing, before taking office and annually thereafter, any personal, financial, political, or business interest that may conflict with KY4Y's objectives or any decision before the Committee.
(c) Where a conflict of interest arises in relation to a specific agenda item, the official with the conflict shall: declare the conflict at the start of that agenda item; absent themselves from the discussion and vote on that item; and have their absence and the reason recorded in the minutes.
(d) The Conflict of Interest Register shall be available for inspection by any member upon written request to the Secretary General.
(e) Failure to declare a known conflict of interest is grounds for disciplinary proceedings under Article 5.

Article 7: Meetings

7.1 Annual General Meeting (AGM)

(a) KY4Y shall hold an Annual General Meeting once every calendar year, not later than 30th June of each year.
(b) The Secretary General shall give members at least 14 days' written notice of the AGM, including the date, time, venue, and agenda.
(c) The AGM shall: receive and approve the annual activity report from the Chairperson; receive and approve the audited financial statements from the Treasurer; elect officials (in election years); set membership subscription fees for the coming year; approve KY4Y's annual work plan and budget; and transact any other business of which proper notice has been given.

7.2 Special General Meetings

(a) The Chairperson may convene a Special General Meeting at any time upon giving 14 days' notice to all members.
(b) The Secretary General shall convene a Special General Meeting upon receipt of a written request signed by at least one-third of members in good standing, within 21 days of receiving such request.
(c) Only the business stated in the notice of a Special General Meeting shall be transacted at that meeting.

7.3 Quorum

(a) The quorum for a General Assembly meeting (AGM or Special) shall be one-third of members in good standing or 20 members, whichever is lesser.
(b) If a quorum is not present within 30 minutes of the scheduled start time, the meeting shall be adjourned to a date not more than 14 days later. At the adjourned meeting, a minimum of 15 members in good standing shall constitute quorum, regardless of total membership size. No adjourned meeting shall proceed with fewer than 15 members present.

7.4 Voting at General Assembly Meetings

(a) Each member in good standing shall have one vote.
(b) Decisions shall be made by simple majority of votes cast, except where this Constitution requires a higher threshold.
(c) The Chairperson shall have a casting vote in the event of a tie (except in elections, where drawing of lots applies).
(d) Voting shall ordinarily be by show of hands. Any member may request a secret ballot, which shall be granted by the Chairperson.
(e) Proxy voting is not permitted.

7.5 Executive Committee Meetings

(a) The Executive Committee shall meet at least once per month.
(b) The quorum for Executive Committee meetings shall be 3 members out of the 5 elected Committee officials.
(c) Decisions shall be by simple majority. The Chairperson has a casting vote in the event of a tie.

Article 8: Financial Management

8.1 Funds of KY4Y

The funds of KY4Y shall consist of: membership subscription fees; donations, grants, and contributions from lawful sources; revenue from KY4Y-approved income-generating activities; and any other funds lawfully received.

8.2 Bank Account

(a) KY4Y shall maintain an official bank account in the name of KASIPUL YOUTH FOR YOUTH MOVEMENT at a reputable bank within Homa Bay County or as approved by the General Assembly.
(b) The bank account shall require the signatures of both of the following officials for any withdrawal or transaction: the Chairperson and the Treasurer.
(c) No single official shall authorize withdrawals alone under any circumstances.

8.3 Use of Funds

(a) KY4Y funds shall only be used for purposes approved by the Executive Committee and consistent with the objectives of this Constitution.
(b) Expenditure between KSh 50,001 and KSh 200,000 shall require prior approval of the Executive Committee. Any expenditure exceeding KSh 200,000 shall require prior approval of the General Assembly. In a declared emergency, the Executive Committee may authorize expenditure above these thresholds, subject to ratification by the appropriate body at its next meeting.
(c) No officer, member, or third party shall receive personal benefit from KY4Y funds except as reasonable compensation for services duly approved by the Executive Committee and documented.

8.4 Financial Records

(a) The Treasurer shall maintain accurate books of accounts recording all income and expenditure of KY4Y.
(b) Financial records shall be retained for a minimum of 7 years.
(c) The Treasurer shall prepare and present a financial report at every Executive Committee meeting and a comprehensive annual financial statement at the AGM.

8.5 Audit

(a) KY4Y's accounts shall be audited annually by an independent auditor appointed by the General Assembly.

Article 9: Records and Reporting

9.1 Member Register

(a) The Secretary General shall maintain an up-to-date Member Register containing for each member: full name; National ID number; date of birth; ward of residence within Kasipul Constituency; phone number and email (where applicable); date of joining; membership tier (Builder / Mentor / Architect / Ambassador); and date of departure or removal (if applicable).
(b) The Member Register shall be available for inspection by the Registering Authority at any time.
(c) A member may inspect their own entry in the Member Register by request to the Secretary General at any time.

9.2 Reports to the Registering Authority

KY4Y shall submit to the Registering Authority:

(a) Half-yearly progress reports covering activities undertaken, membership changes, and financial summary — due by 31st July (for January–June) and 31st January (for July–December of the preceding year).
(b) Annual audited financial statements — within 6 months of the end of each financial year.
(c) Amended constitution — within 1 month of any constitutional amendment.
(d) Updated list of officials — within 1 month of any change in Executive Committee membership.

9.3 Access to Records

KY4Y shall grant the Registering Authority access to all records, documents, and facilities upon request, as required by the Act.

Article 10: Amendments to this Constitution

10.1 This Constitution may be amended by a resolution passed by at least two-thirds (2/3) of members present and voting at a General Assembly meeting.

10.2 Notice of any proposed amendment shall be given to all members at least 21 days before the meeting at which it is to be considered. The notice shall include the full text of the proposed amendment.
10.3 No amendment shall be made that is inconsistent with the Community Groups Registration Act No. 30 of 2022 or any other written law of Kenya.
10.4 Any amendment to this Constitution shall be submitted to the Registering Authority within 1 month of adoption, together with the amended constitution.

Article 11: Disputes and Conflict Resolution

11.1 KY4Y shall seek to resolve internal disputes through dialogue and mediation before escalating to formal proceedings.
11.2 Any dispute between members, or between a member and the Executive Committee, shall first be referred to the Chairperson for mediation. If the Chairperson is a party to the dispute, the Secretary General shall mediate.
11.3 If mediation fails, the dispute shall be referred to the General Assembly, whose decision shall be final on internal matters.
11.4 Nothing in this Article shall prevent a member from exercising any legal right available to them under Kenyan law.

Article 12: Dissolution

12.1 KY4Y may be dissolved by a resolution passed by at least three-quarters (3/4) of all members in good standing at a Special General Meeting convened specifically for that purpose, with at least 21 days' notice given to all members.
12.2 Upon dissolution, after payment of all debts and liabilities, the remaining assets of KY4Y shall not be distributed among members but shall be transferred to one or more organizations registered in Kasipul with similar objectives, as determined by the General Assembly at the dissolution meeting.
12.3 The Executive Committee shall notify the Registering Authority of the dissolution within 14 days of the General Assembly resolution.

Article 13: General Provisions

13.1 Compliance with Law — KY4Y shall comply with the Community Groups Registration Act No. 30 of 2022 and all other applicable laws of Kenya at all times.
13.2 Non-Distribution — No part of KY4Y's income or assets shall be distributed to or for the personal benefit of any member, officer, or associate, except as reasonable and approved compensation for services rendered.
13.3 Official Seal and Logo — KY4Y may adopt an official seal and logo. The use of the KY4Y name, seal, and logo shall be regulated by the Executive Committee.
13.4 Indemnity — KY4Y shall indemnify officials and members acting in good faith in the lawful discharge of their duties from personal liability arising from such acts.

Article 14: Honorary Patron and Founding Patrons

14.1 Honorary Patron

The serving Member of Parliament for Kasipul Constituency is hereby invited to serve as the Honorary Patron of KY4Y. The inaugural Honorary Patron is the Honourable Boyd Were, MP for Kasipul Constituency.

14.2 The Honorary Patron shall:
  • Represent the formal relationship between KY4Y and Kasipul Constituency's elected parliamentary leadership
  • Champion KY4Y's objectives before Parliament, government institutions, and the private sector
  • Attend and address General Assembly meetings and official KY4Y events upon invitation
  • Lend their name, voice, and platform to advance the interests and visibility of KY4Y and the youth of Kasipul
14.3 The Honorary Patron shall have no voting rights, no financial authority, no executive function, and no power of appointment or dismissal within KY4Y. The Honorary Patron may not attend Executive Committee meetings unless specifically invited by the Chairperson for a defined advisory purpose.
14.4 The Honorary Patron role is attached to the office of Member of Parliament for Kasipul Constituency, not to any individual. Upon a change in constituency representation, the Executive Committee shall formally invite the incoming MP to continue in the role of Honorary Patron.

14.5 Founding Patrons

14.5 KY4Y may recognise individuals who have made significant contributions to the founding or early growth of the Movement as Founding Patrons.
14.6 Founding Patron status shall be conferred by a resolution of the Executive Committee and recorded in the Movement's official register.
14.7 Founding Patrons shall be recognised at official KY4Y events, listed in KY4Y's public communications, and may be invited to address the General Assembly at the discretion of the Executive Committee.
14.8 Founding Patron status carries no voting rights, no financial authority, no executive function, and no power of appointment within KY4Y. Founding Patrons may not attend Executive Committee meetings unless specifically invited by the Chairperson for a defined advisory purpose.
14.9 Founding Patron status may be revoked by the Executive Committee for conduct inconsistent with KY4Y's values or for any action that brings the Movement into disrepute.
