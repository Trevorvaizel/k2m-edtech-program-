<a name="program-philosophy"></a>
## 2. PROGRAM PHILOSOPHY & GUARDRAILS

### WHAT THIS COHORT ACTUALLY IS

**We are NOT:**
- Teaching AI tools or prompts
- Certifying technical skills
- Creating portfolio projects
- Teaching "advanced" AI systems
- A traditional course

**We ARE:**
- Moving learners through **perceptual zones** (Zone 0 → early Zone 4)
- Installing **durable thinking habits**
- Creating **identity shifts** ("AI is for me" → "I work with AI intentionally")
- Designing for **psychological safety**, not performance
- Building **confidence that transfers** to university and SME contexts

### THE ZONE PROGRESSION (INTERNAL FRAMEWORK)

**Zone 0:** "AI is not for me / it's sci-fi"
→ **Zone 1:** "AI is something I could use"
→ **Zone 2:** "AI does tasks for me"
→ **Zone 3:** "AI understands my intent / I collaborate with it"
→ **Zone 4:** "I control quality / I direct AI"

**Our Target (6 Weeks):** Zone 0 → early Zone 4

### DESIGN GUARDRAILS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**We NEVER:**
- Use technical jargon in Week 1
- Show "impressive" AI outputs (creates intimidation)
- Compare students or rank outputs
- Praise speed or "correctness"
- Talk about "building," "automation," or "systems" (premature for Zone 0-2)
- Evaluate or grade artifacts

**We ALWAYS:**
- Prioritize clarity over cleverness
- Prioritize confidence over competence
- Normalize confusion ("this is normal")
- Use repetition over novelty
- Create psychological safety
- Frame AI as responding to **thinking**, not intelligence

### THE 4 CORE HABITS WE INSTALL

By Week 6, students will have these habits:

1. **Pause before asking** → "What do I actually want?"
2. **Explain context first** → "Here's my situation..."
3. **Change one thing at a time** → "What does this affect?"
4. **Use AI before decisions** → "Help me think, not just answer"

If they keep these habits, the cohort succeeded. Tools change, habits transfer.

### WEEK-BY-WEEK PROGRESSION

| Week | Zone Shift | Public Promise | Student Feels | Habit Installed |
|------|-----------|----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 0→2 | "AI will feel less random" | "I can use this" | Pause before asking |
| 2 | 2→3 | "You'll understand why AI works sometimes" | "I see patterns" | Explain context |
| 3 | 3 | "You'll use AI to think, not just answer" | "This is a thinking partner" | Use before decisions |

| 6 | Early 4 | "You'll have proof of your new approach" | "I'm ready for uni" | All habits stabilized |

### ⚠️ JOURNEY COMPRESSION NOTE

**What This Cohort Combines:**

This 6-week cohort combines TWO journeys from the AI Curriculum Framework:
- **Journey A:** Zone 0 → 2 (typically 2-4 weeks)
- **Journey B:** Zone 2 → 4 (typically 6-8 weeks)

**Standard Timeline:** 8-12 weeks total
**Our Timeline:** 6 weeks (accelerated)

**Why This Compression Works:**
1. **Intensive Facilitation:** 8 moderators for 200 students (1:25 ratio) creates high-touch support
2. **Daily Practice:** 15-20 min/day maintains momentum between sessions
3. **Cohort Momentum:** 200 students learning together creates accelerated growth

**Risks We're Managing:**
- **Integration Time:** Foundation says "Rushing creates unstable shifts." We address this through:
  - Week 4-6 are explicitly "consolidation weeks" (no new content)
  - Daily practice allows gradual integration, not cramming
  - Reflection prompts stabilize learning

- **Overwhelm Risk:** Week 1 combines Zone 0→1 AND Zone 1→2 shifts (ambitious). We manage this by:
  - Facilitators monitor for overwhelm signals
  - Week 1 session is 90 minutes (not rushed)
  - Diagnostic identifies students who may need extra support

- **Quality Maintenance:** Faster pace = quality risk. We maintain quality through:
  - Pre-cohort diagnostic (SOP 0) ensures we meet students where they are
  - Small clusters (25 students) prevent anyone from getting lost
  - Facilitator training emphasizes "notice struggle, don't rescue"

**If Compression Isn't Working:**
If a student is overwhelmed after Week 1-2, facilitators can:
1. Slow them down (focus on current zone, don't push)
2. Offer 1:1 support session
3. Emphasize that Zone 2 is a valid stopping point (Journey A complete)

**Our Commitment:** We prioritize deep learning over speed. If 50% of students reach Zone 4 and 50% reach solid Zone 3, that's better than 100% reaching shaky Zone 4.

### DISCORD RULES (PINNED)

**Learners CAN:**
- Post practices
- Reflect
- Ask themselves questions
- React with emojis

**Learners CANNOT:**
- Critique others
- Give advice
- Optimize prompts
- Debate approaches
- Answer other learners' questions

**Why:** Keeps Zone 3 learners from dominating Zone 0s. Prevents "expert" performance culture.

**Lead Facilitator (You):**
- Full server access
- Posts weekly intent

- Daily rhythm-keeping (15-20 min/day)
- Post daily practice prompts
- Acknowledge reflections with emojis
- Nudge silent learners privately
- Flag disengagement early

**Moderators NEVER:**
- Explain concepts
- Correct thinking
- Optimize outputs
- "Teach" in Discord

### CLUSTER ASSIGNMENT

**Total:** 200 students
**Clusters:** 8 groups of 25
**Each cluster has:** 1 moderator

**Assignment Logic:** Diagnostic zone + time zone preference

**IMPORTANT:** All students complete SOP 0 (Pre-Cohort Diagnostic) before cluster assignment. This ensures:
- Similar-zone students are grouped together (Zone 0 students together, Zone 1 students together)
- Facilitators know each student's starting barrier type
- Baseline data is captured for progression measurement

## 5. WEEK-BY-WEEK SESSION SCRIPTS

### STANDARD SESSION FORMAT (90 MINUTES)
1. Arrival & Framing (10 min)
2. Pattern Reveal (15 min)
3. Guided Practice (20 min)
4. Reflection & Naming (20 min)
5. Week Framing + Close (10 min)

**Facilitator Tone:** Calm, curious, non-performative, non-authoritative

---

### WEEK 1 SESSION SCRIPT: "WHY AI FEELS CONFUSING"

**0-10 min: Arrival & Framing**
```
"Welcome! This is not a course about AI tools.
It's about how you approach thinking with AI.

Most people think AI is inconsistent.
What you'll discover today is that it's actually very consistent —
just very literal.

You don't need to be 'technical' to be here.
If you can explain something to another human, you already have what you need."
```

**10-25 min: First Contact (Zone 0 → 1)**
```
"Let's start with something very simple.

Ask AI for help with something you actually care about right now.
NOT for school. NOT for productivity.
Just something real in your life.

Examples:
- Help me think about how to spend my break
- Explain something I've always been confused about
- Help me decide between two options

Don't try to be smart. Just ask."
```

[Wait 5 minutes for them to try]

**25-40 min: The Contrast (Zone 1 → 2)**
```
"Now let me show you something.

[Display same task, two ways]

Version A: 'Help me plan my first semester at university'
Version B: 'I'm starting university in February, I'm nervous about managing
my time because I've never lived independently before. Can you help me think
through what I should focus on in my first semester?'

Notice the difference?
Which one feels like it actually understood you?"
```

[Let them respond]

**40-60 min: Guided Redo**
```
"Take the same thing you just asked AI about.
Ask again — but this time, explain it like you're talking to a helpful person.

Include:
- Your situation
- What you're trying to do
- Why it matters to you

Don't optimize. Don't make it clever. Just explain."
```

[Wait 10 minutes]

**60-75 min: Silent Comparison**
```
"Compare your two responses.

Ask yourself:
What changed when you explained more?

Don't share the answer. Just notice it."
```

**75-90 min: Anchor + Close**
```
"Here's what I want you to leave with today:

AI didn't change in the last 90 minutes.
You crossed a threshold.

When AI feels confusing, don't assume it's broken.
Ask: What context am I missing?

This week, you'll practice this on things you actually do.
Not because you have to.
Because once you see this, you can't unsee it.

See you in Discord."
```

---

### WEEK 1 ASYNC PRACTICE

**Posted in `#daily-practice`:**
```
PRACTICE 1: The Explanations Test

Choose one thing you'll do this week anyway.
Ask AI for help twice:

1. Quickly, without explaining
2. Again, but explain your situation first

Post your reflection (1-2 lines only):
"What changed when you explained more?"

No sharing outputs. No comparing. Just your reflection.
```

---

### WEEK 2 SESSION: "WHY SMALL CHANGES MATTER"

**0-10 min: Normalization**
```
"Last week, AI started feeling less random.

This week, you'll understand WHY it works sometimes —
and why it doesn't other times.

Most people think they're 'bad at AI' when results change.
Today you'll see that you're the variable — and that's good news."
```

**10-30 min: The Small Change Demo**
```
[Show one task, same wording, ONE detail changed]

Same request.
Only difference: who it's for / the goal / the situation.

Notice what changed in the output.
```

**30-50 min: Pattern Recognition**
```
"Here's the rule:

AI doesn't guess what matters.
It uses whatever you give it to decide.

Change one thing → output changes.
That's not inconsistency. That's control."
```

**50-75 min: Individual Practice**
```
"Reuse last week's task.
Change only ONE detail:
- Who is this for?
- What's the goal?
- What's my situation?

Compare the two outputs silently.
Ask: Which change mattered most?
```

**75-90 min: Anchor**
```
"When results change, don't restart.
Change one thing and watch what happens.

This is how bad habits are prevented.
This is how control begins."
```

---

### WEEK 2 ASYNC PRACTICE
```
PRACTICE 2: The One-Thing Test

Take one request you made this week.
Change only ONE detail and observe the result.

Reflection (1-2 lines):
"What detail mattered most?"
```

---

### WEEK 3 SESSION: "AI AS THINKING PARTNER"

**0-15 min: Reframe**
```
"So far, you've learned:
- AI responds when you explain
- Small changes create big differences

This week, you'll use AI differently —
not to get answers, but to think.

AI is not just a tool that gives you things.
It can help you see your own thinking more clearly."
```

**15-35 min: Mirror Demo**
```
[Demonstrate asking AI to:]
- Explain your thinking back to you
- Challenge an assumption
- Simplify something you wrote
- Show you another perspective

Key: AI is responding to thoughts, not replacing them.
```

**35-60 min: Thinking Task**
```
"Choose:
- A decision you're stuck on
- An idea you're developing
- A plan you're making
- Something you're confused about

Ask AI to:
- Clarify it
- Challenge it
- Restructure it
- Show you what you're missing

NO right answers. NO sharing outputs."
```

**60-80 min: Naming**
```
"Notice something:

You weren't asking for answers.
You were asking for perspective.

That's a different relationship with AI."
```

**80-90 min: Anchor**
```
"When you're stuck, AI is most useful BEFORE you decide —
not after.

This is how it becomes a thinking partner.
This is how you stop being alone with hard problems."
```

---

### WEEK 3 ASYNC PRACTICE
```
PRACTICE 3: The Before-Decision Test

Use AI to help you think through something unclear —
NOT to finish it.

Reflection:
"How did it change how I see the problem?"
```

## 6. FACILITATOR TRAINING & SOPs

**Who Makes a Good Facilitator:**
- Calm presence (not performers)
- Comfortable with NOT answering questions
- Can follow a script without improvising
- 15-20 min/day availability for Discord
- Reliable internet connection

**Who Does NOT:**
- People who love showing off what they know
- People who can't resist "fixing" student outputs
- People who need to be the smartest person in the room

### 2-WEEK FACILITATOR TRAINING

**Week 1: Experience the Cohort**
- [ ] Go through the 6-week experience as a participant
- [ ] Practice NOT answering
- [ ] Learn intervention rules
- [ ] Read this entire playbook

**Week 2: Shadow + Practice**
- [ ] Shadow 2 live sessions
- [ ] Run 1 session with supervision
- [ ] Debrief language use
- [ ] Get signed off as ready

### FACILITATOR DAILY RHYTHM (15-20 MIN/DAY)

**Daily Duties:**
1. Post the daily practice prompt (copy from templates)
2. Acknowledge reflections with emojis only (❤️, 🔥, 💡, ✅)
3. Nudge silent learners privately (use template)
4. Flag any disengagement to you

**Weekly Duties:**
1. Post cluster check-in prompt
2. Summarize ENERGY (not content)
3. Escalate concerns

### FACILITATOR INTERVENTION RULES

**When TO Engage:**
- Technical confusion (how to use Discord)
- Emotional distress
- Someone hasn't posted in 3+ days

**When NOT TO Engage:**
- "Is my prompt good?" (Let them experience)
- "Can someone help me improve this?" (Not allowed)
- Tool questions during practice
- Debates about approaches

**What TO Say:**
- "Notice what happened there."
- "That reaction makes sense."
- "What do you think changed?"
- "You don't need to understand this fully yet."

**What NOT TO Say:**
- "The right way to do this is..."
- "Here's a better prompt..."
- "You should try..."
- "Actually, it works better if..."

### MODERATOR SCRIPTS

**Nudge Script (for silent learners):**
```
"Hey [name]! Haven't seen you in the cluster this week.
Everything okay? No pressure to post — just checking in."
```

**Rule Reminder Script:**
```
"Quick reminder: In this channel, we share reflections but don't
give feedback or advice. Everyone's learning at their own pace.

Emoji reactions are great! Let's keep it there."
```

**Escalation Template (to you):**

## 11. OPERATIONAL SOPs

### SOP 0: PRE-COHORT DIAGNOSTIC ⚠️ CRITICAL

**Purpose:** Before any content, know where they are. You cannot design a journey without knowing the starting point.

**When:** After payment confirmed, before Discord invite sent.

**Who Administers:** Facilitator or trained admin (15-20 minutes)

---

#### DIAGNOSTIC CONVERSATION GUIDE (15 MINUTES)

**Format:** Video call (Zoom/Google Meet) OR structured Google Form

**Opening (2 minutes):**
```
"Thanks for joining! Before we start the cohort, I want to understand
where you're at with AI so we can support you best.

This isn't a test. There are no right answers. I just want to know
your experience so far.

Everything you share helps us place you in the right cluster
and prepare our facilitators.

Ready?"
```

**Phase 1: Open (2 min)**
```
"Tell me about your experience with AI so far.

Have you used ChatGPT or Claude? What for?
How did it go?
What's your honest feeling about AI right now?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Language patterns (zone indicators below)
- Energy (excitement, fear, confusion)
- Specificity (vague = lower zone)

**Phase 2: Scenario Response (5 min)**
```
"Let me give you a situation.

You need to write an important email for a scholarship application,
or maybe a personal statement for university. You have ChatGPT available.

Walk me through what you'd do."
```

**Their Response Map:**
| Response Pattern | Likely Zone | What It Means |
|------------------|-------------|---------------|
| "I'd write it myself" | 0-1 | AI not part of their process |
| "I'd ask ChatGPT to write it" | 2 | Delegates task to AI |
| "I'd explain my situation first, then work with ChatGPT" | 3 | Understands collaboration |
| "I'd start with a draft, then iterate with AI to improve it" | 4 | Directs quality intentionally |

**Phase 3: Experience Check (3 min)**

Ask these questions IN ORDER. Where they stop saying "yes" = their zone:

1. "Have you used ChatGPT or Claude at all?"
   - No = Zone 0

2. "Have you used it for actual work or real tasks (not just testing)?"
   - No = Zone 1

3. "Have you had a back-and-forth conversation with AI to refine something?"
   - No = Zone 2

4. "Have you spent 10+ minutes improving one AI output by giving it feedback?"
   - No = Zone 3

5. "Have you created something custom with AI (like a custom GPT or automation)?"
   - No = Zone 4

**Phase 4: Barrier Identification (3 min)**
```
"What's stopped you from going further with AI?

Be honest - what comes up for you?"
```

**Listen for Barrier Types:**
- **Identity:** "I'm not technical" / "I'm not a tech person"
- **Trust:** "Can you trust it?" / "What if it's wrong?"
- **Skill:** "It doesn't understand me" / "I don't know what to ask"
- **Patience:** "Good enough is fine" / "Who has time for that?"
- **Vision:** "I wouldn't know how to build anything"

**Phase 5: Destination (2 min)**
```
"What would you want to be able to do with AI that you can't do now?

What's your goal for the next 6 weeks?"
```

This reveals:
- Motivation
- Expectations
- How far they want to go

#### WHY THIS MATTERS (Foundational Alignment)

**From AI Curriculum Framework, Part 2:**
> "Before any content: know where they are. You cannot design a journey
> without knowing the starting point."

**What This Prevents:**
1. **One-Size-Fits-All Teaching:** Zone 0 students need different support than Zone 2 students
2. **Missed Opportunities:** We don't know who needs extra help until it's too late
3. **Poor Placement:** Random cluster assignment mixes zones, creates uneven experiences

**What This Enables:**
1. **Targeted Support:** Facilitators know each student's barrier type upfront
2. **Baseline Data:** We can measure actual zone progression (not just assume it)
3. **Cluster Balance:** Ensure each cluster has similar zone distribution

**Time Investment:** 15-20 min × 200 students = 50-67 hours total
**Value Returned:** Significant. This is the difference between "teaching at them" and "meeting them where they are."

---

#### CLUSTER ASSIGNMENT LOGIC (After Diagnostic)

**Principle:** Group similar zones together, but ensure diversity of barrier types.

**Example Cluster (25 students):**
- 15 students at Zone 0-1 (need activation)
- 8 students at Zone 2 (ready for collaboration)
- 2 students at Zone 3 (can model, but not dominate)

**Barrier Type Mix:**
- Identity barriers: ~30%
- Trust barriers: ~25%
- Skill barriers: ~25%
- Other: ~20%

**Why This Mix Works:**
- Zone 0-1 students aren't intimidated by Zone 3 students (only 2)
- Zone 3 students can model without dominating
- Barrier diversity means facilitators see multiple resistance types

**Red Flags (Reassign Student):**
- More than 3 Zone 3 students in one cluster (they'll dominate)
- More than 20 students with same barrier type (facilitator overload)
- Time zone incompatible (can't attend sessions)

- Where student places responsibility (AI vs. self)
- How they explain failure (bad AI vs. missing context)
- Whether they iterate (one-and-done vs. refinement)

**3. Engagement Signals**
- Attendance at live sessions
- Practice completion rate
- Reflection depth (not quality)
- Peer acknowledgment given

**4. Confidence Statements**
- "I feel more confident using AI"
- "I understand why things work now"
- "I'm not afraid to use AI"

### SHIFT VERIFICATION QUESTIONS ⚠️ CRITICAL

**Foundational Alignment:** From AI Curriculum Framework, Part 6 (Assessment System)

These questions are NOT for grading. They're for detecting perceptual shifts.

**How to Use:**
- Facilitators ask these questions during weekly check-ins (Week 1, 2, 3, 6)
- Listen for zone-indicative language (not right/wrong answers)
- Record student's zone progression in tracking sheet

---

#### ZONE 0→1 VERIFICATION (Week 1 Check-In)

**The Shift:** "AI is not for me" → "AI is something I could use"

**Verification Question:**
> "What would you want to try with AI?"

**Listen For:**

| Not Shifted (Zone 0) | Shifted (Zone 1) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "I don't know" / "Nothing" | Has specific ideas |
| "AI is for other people" | "I should try that" |
| Dismissive, distant | Curious, interested |
| "I'm not technical" | "I could learn this" |

**Language Shifts to Notice:**
- Before: "That AI stuff" / "Not for me"
- After: "I could use AI for..." / "I want to try..."

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Zone 0 students need permission to experiment
- Create safe, low-stakes opportunities (no judgment)
- Normalize "I tried and it was weird" experiences

---

#### ZONE 1→2 VERIFICATION (Week 1-2 Check-In)

**The Shift:** "AI is interesting" → "AI does real work for me"

**Verification Question:**
> "Tell me about the last time AI helped you with something."

**Listen For:**

| Not Shifted (Zone 1) | Shifted (Zone 2) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| Vague or no example | Specific recent example |
| "I should try..." | "I used it for..." |
| Experimental use | Regular use for real tasks |
| Skeptical of output | Trusts (with verification) |

**Language Shifts to Notice:**
- Before: "I should try..." / "It's interesting"
- After: "I used it for..." / "It helped me with..."

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Zone 1→2 shift happens when AI becomes practical (not novel)
- Look for "real task" examples (email, homework, planning)
- Integration into workflow is the signal

---

#### ZONE 2→3 VERIFICATION (Week 2-3 Check-In)

**The Shift:** "AI does tasks" → "AI understands my intent"

**Verification Question:**
> "Walk me through how you'd use AI for [complex task like planning your semester]."

**Listen For:**

| Not Shifted (Zone 2) | Shifted (Zone 3) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "I'd ask it to [simple command]" | "I'd first explain..." |
| One-shot usage | Multi-turn refinement |
| "AI didn't understand" | "I need to explain better" |
| Task execution | Thinking partnership |

**Language Shifts to Notice:**
- Before: "I'd ask ChatGPT to..."
- After: "I'd explain my situation, then we'd work through..."

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Zone 2→3 shift is about conversation, not commands
- Look for multi-turn conversations (back-and-forth)
- Students notice "AI gets me when I explain more"

---

#### ZONE 3→4 VERIFICATION (Week 6 Check-In)

**The Shift:** "AI understands me" → "I control the quality"

**Verification Question:**
> "How do you know when AI output is done? When do you stop iterating?"

**Listen For:**

| Not Shifted (Zone 3) | Shifted (Zone 4) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "When it makes sense" | "When it meets my criteria" |
| "When it's good enough" | "When I've shaped it to be excellent" |
| Waits for AI to get it right | Actively directs quality |
| Accepts first-draft output | Iterates deliberately |

**Language Shifts to Notice:**
- Before: "AI gave me this" / "It looks good"
- After: "I made this with AI" / "I refined it until..."

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Zone 3→4 shift is about ownership, not collaboration
- Look for deliberate iteration (5+ turns on same output)
- Students say "I directed this" not "AI gave me this"
