## ✅ FIX 1: PRE-COHORT DIAGNOSTIC SYSTEM (SOP 0)

**What Was Added:**
- Complete SOP 0: Pre-Cohort Diagnostic (15 pages)
- 15-minute diagnostic conversation guide
- Google Form alternative (for scale)
- Cluster assignment logic based on diagnostic results
- Baseline data capture for progression measurement

**Where It Lives:**
- Section 11 (Operational SOPs) → SOP 0 (now first SOP)
- Also referenced in: Execution checklist, Enrollment funnel, SOP 1

**Why This Matters (Foundational Alignment):**

**From AI Curriculum Framework, Part 2:**
> "Before any content: know where they are. You cannot design a journey
> without knowing the starting point."

**What This Prevents:**
1. **One-Size-Fits-All Teaching:** Zone 0 students need different support than Zone 2 students
2. **Missed Opportunities:** We don't know who needs extra help until it's too late
3. **Poor Placement:** Random cluster assignment mixes zones, creates uneven experiences

**What This Enables:**
1. **Targeted Support:** Facilitators know each student's barrier type upfront
2. **Baseline Data:** We can measure actual zone progression (not just assume it)
3. **Cluster Balance:** Ensure each cluster has similar zone distribution

**Implementation Details:**

**5-Phase Diagnostic Conversation:**
1. **Open (2 min):** "Tell me about your experience with AI so far."
2. **Scenario Response (5 min):** "Walk me through how you'd use AI for [task]."
3. **Experience Check (3 min):** Ask 5 questions IN ORDER to identify zone
4. **Barrier Identification (3 min):** "What's stopped you from going further?"
5. **Destination (2 min):** "What would you want to be able to do?"

**Diagnostic Output Captured:**
- Current Zone (0-7)
- Barrier Type (identity/trust/skill/patience/vision)
- Barrier Strength (light/medium/strong)
- Destination Interest
- Readiness (resistant/open/eager)

**Time Investment:** 15-20 min × 200 students = 50-67 hours total
**Value Returned:** Critical. This is the difference between "teaching at them" and "meeting them where they are."

## ✅ FIX 2: JOURNEY COMPRESSION NOTE

**What Was Added:**
- Explicit acknowledgment that 6-week cohort compresses TWO journeys (Journey A + Journey B)
- Explanation of why compression works (intensive facilitation, daily practice, cohort momentum)
- Risks we're managing (integration time, overwhelm risk, quality maintenance)
- Exit strategies if compression isn't working for specific students

**Where It Lives:**
- Section 3 (6-Week Cohort Design) → New subsection after Week-by-Week Progression table

**Why This Matters (Foundational Alignment):**

**From AI Curriculum Framework:**
- **Journey A:** Zone 0 → 2 (typically 2-4 weeks)
- **Journey B:** Zone 2 → 4 (typically 6-8 weeks)
- **Standard Timeline:** 8-12 weeks total
- **Our Timeline:** 6 weeks (accelerated)

**Risk Acknowledged:**
Foundation says: "Rushing creates unstable shifts that collapse."

**How We're Managing It:**

**1. Integration Time Risk:**
- Week 4-6 are explicitly "consolidation weeks" (no new content)
- Daily practice allows gradual integration, not cramming
- Reflection prompts stabilize learning

**2. Overwhelm Risk (Week 1 combines Zone 0→1 AND Zone 1→2):**
- Facilitators monitor for overwhelm signals
- Week 1 session is 90 minutes (not rushed)
- Diagnostic identifies students who may need extra support

**3. Quality Maintenance Risk:**
- Pre-cohort diagnostic (SOP 0) ensures we meet students where they are
- Small clusters (25 students) prevent anyone from getting lost
- Facilitator training emphasizes "notice struggle, don't rescue"

**Exit Strategy (If Compression Isn't Working):**
If a student is overwhelmed after Week 1-2, facilitators can:
1. Slow them down (focus on current zone, don't push)
2. Offer 1:1 support session
3. Emphasize that Zone 2 is a valid stopping point (Journey A complete)

**Our Commitment:**
"We prioritize deep learning over speed. If 50% of students reach Zone 4 and 50% reach solid Zone 3, that's better than 100% reaching shaky Zone 4."

## ✅ FIX 3: SHIFT VERIFICATION QUESTIONS

**What Was Added:**
- Specific verification questions for each zone transition (0→1, 1→2, 2→3, 3→4)
- "Not shifted" vs "Shifted" comparison tables
- Language shifts to listen for
- Facilitator notes for each transition
- Weekly check-in scripts (Week 1, 2, 3, 6)
- Progression tracking sheet template

**Where It Lives:**
- Section 12 (Measurement & Reporting) → New subsection "SHIFT VERIFICATION QUESTIONS"
- Integrated into: Facilitator check-ins, progression tracking

**Why This Matters (Foundational Alignment):**

**From AI Curriculum Framework, Part 6 (Assessment System):**
> "Not 'did they complete the exercises' but 'did their perception change?'"

**What This Provides:**

**Zone 0→1 Verification (Week 1):**
- **Question:** "What would you want to try with AI?"
- **Shifted:** Has specific ideas, curious, interested
- **Not Shifted:** "I don't know" / "Nothing"

**Zone 1→2 Verification (Week 1-2):**
- **Question:** "Tell me about the last time AI helped you with something."
- **Shifted:** Specific recent example, real task, trusts output
- **Not Shifted:** Vague, experimental, skeptical

**Zone 2→3 Verification (Week 2-3):**
- **Question:** "Walk me through how you'd use AI for [complex task]."
- **Shifted:** Explains first, multi-turn refinement, "I need to explain better"
- **Not Shifted:** One-shot command, "AI didn't understand"

**Zone 3→4 Verification (Week 6):**
- **Question:** "How do you know when AI output is done? When do you stop iterating?"
- **Shifted:** "When it meets my criteria", actively directs, deliberate iteration
- **Not Shifted:** "When it's good enough", waits for AI, accepts first draft

**Weekly Facilitator Check-In Scripts:**

**Week 1:** "What would you want to try with AI that you haven't tried yet?"
**Week 2:** "Think about the last time AI helped you with something real. What was it?"
**Week 3:** "What feels different about how you approach AI compared to Week 1?"
**Week 6:** "How do you know when AI output is done? When do you stop working on it?"

**Progression Tracking:**
For each student, track weekly:
- Week 1: Asks "what to try" questions, uses AI for real task, current zone
- Week 2: Explains context, notices output changes, current zone
- Week 3: Has multi-turn conversation, uses AI for thinking, current zone
- Week 6: Iterates deliberately, says "I made this", final zone
