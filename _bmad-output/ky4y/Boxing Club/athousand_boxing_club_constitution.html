A Thousand Boxing Club - Constitution Articles 1-4

Article 1: Name

The name of the organization shall be A Thousand Boxing Club - Oyugis.

Article 2: Area of Operation

The club shall operate within Homa Bay County and Kenya as a whole.

Article 3: Objectives

The objectives of the club shall be:

  1. To promote boxing and physical fitness among youth.
  2. To identify and nurture boxing talent in the community.
  3. To encourage discipline, teamwork, and sportsmanship.
  4. To support members through training, mentorship and organized sports activities.
  5. To mobilize savings and manage a loan system for the benefit of members.

Article 4: Membership

4.1 Eligibility

Membership shall be open to individuals interested in boxing and community sports development who agree to abide by this constitution.

4.2 Membership Requirements

A member shall:

  • Register with the club.
  • Pay the agreed membership fee.
  • Participate in club activities.

4.3 Termination of Membership

Membership may be suspended or terminated if a member:

  • Violates the constitution.
  • Misuses club funds or property.
  • Acts in a manner that damages the reputation of the club.

  • Maintain correspondence and documentation of the club.

Article 7: Meetings

7.1 Regular Meetings

The club shall hold regular meetings as agreed by members.

7.2 Annual General Meeting (AGM)

An Annual General Meeting shall be held once every year to review club activities and finances.

7.3 Quorum

The quorum for meetings shall be at least half of the registered members.

Article 8: Finance

8.1 Sources of Funds

The funds of the club shall come from:

  • Membership registration fees
  • Member contributions
  • Donations or sponsorships
  • Competition or activity income

Article 8: Finance (continued)

8.2 Bank Account

The club shall operate a bank or group account.

Signatories to the account shall include:

  • Chairman
  • Treasurer
  • Secretary

Any withdrawal shall require at least two signatories.

Article 9: Savings and Loans

9.1 Purpose

The club may operate a savings and loan system to support members financially and grow club funds.

9.2 Savings

Members may contribute savings as agreed during meetings. All savings shall be recorded by the treasurer.

9.3 Loan Eligibility

A member may qualify for a loan if:

  • They have been an active member of the club.
  • They have savings within the club.
  • The loan is approved by the Executive Committee.

9.4 Loan Limit

A member may borrow an amount not exceeding three times their savings, subject to approval by the committee.

9.5 Loan Approval

Loans must be approved by the Chairman, Secretary, and Treasurer and recorded in the club loan register.

9.6 Repayment

Loans shall be repaid within a period agreed by the members during approval.

Failure to repay may result in deductions from the member's savings or disciplinary action by the committee.

Article 10: Elections

Elections of office bearers shall be held during the Annual General Meeting.

Voting shall be conducted by secret ballot and a simple majority shall determine the winner.

Article 11: Amendment of the Constitution

This constitution may be amended during a meeting of members.

Any amendment must be approved by two-thirds of members present.

Article 12: Dissolution

In the event that the club is dissolved:

  • Members shall be notified at least 30 days in advance.
  • All debts and liabilities shall first be cleared.
  • Remaining assets shall be shared among members according to their contributions or as decided by the members.
