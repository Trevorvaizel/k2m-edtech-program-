## SECTION 2: PRE-MORTEM ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Capital Starvation - The Primary Threat

**Failure Scenario:**
By September 2026, we've burned through initial goodwill without revenue. Alumni are fatigued. Grassroots partners feel "used again." We become another NGO that promised transformation but delivered nothing sustainable.

**Why It Happens:**
- Optimized for "impact" (visibility, numbers) not "sustainability" (revenue, infrastructure)
- No clear revenue models in first 12 months
- Donor-funded mindset instead of business-model mindset

**How We Prevent It:**
- **Rule #1:** Every portfolio must have revenue model from Day 1
- **Rule #2:** No "we'll figure out monetization later"
- **Rule #3:** Monthly cash-flow projections from registration
- **Rule #4:** If portfolio doesn't reach cash-flow positive by Month 18 → kill it

**Revenue Targets:**
- **Festival:** KSh 1.5M revenue by April 25 (break-even on KSh 1.2M cost)
- **Konnekt:** KSh 200K/month by Month 12
- **Credentialing:** KSh 50K/month by Month 12
- **Investment Circle:** KSh 1M fund raised by Month 6

**Total Runway Needed:** KSh 5M for 18 months → KSh 280K/month covered by Month 12

**If We Don't Hit Targets:** Pause scaling, focus on revenue-generating activities only

---

### 2.2 Political Backlash - The Poison Working Too Well

**Failure Scenario:**
Both political parties view us as threat. They coordinate to disrupt. Media attacks. Grassroots partners are pressured to disassociate. We become "that youth organization causing trouble."

**Why It Happens:**
- We positioned as "political poison" but didn't build immunity first
- Visible before being indispensable
- Seen as supporting opposition by virtue of being "neutral"

**How We Prevent It:**
- **Rule #1:** Boring infrastructure until indispensable (can't become campaign slogan)
- **Rule #2:** No public political statements (focus on service, not politics)
- **Rule #3:** Grassroots partnerships are with TALENT PIPELINES, not political wards
- **Rule #4:** Distribute power (no single leader to target)

**Immunity Building:**
- **Months 1-6:** Zero political visibility
- **Months 7-12:** Only local constituency visibility (service delivery)
- **Months 13-18:** Regional visibility (still boring infrastructure)
- **Month 19+:** National visibility (only after indispensable)

**The Boyd Were Principle Applied:**
"Invisible until inevitable. Essential before visible."

---

### 2.3 The Two Rivers Narrative Collapse

**Failure Scenario:**
Grassroots partners feel alumni came in, took credit, left. Alumni feel undervalued after funding everything. Narrative becomes "co-option" not "convergence."

**Why It Happens:**
- Visual hierarchy had alumni brands first
- Revenue share didn't prioritize grassroots
- Alumni spoke FOR grassroots instead of WITH them
- "While we were away, you built" was rhetoric, not reality

**How We Prevent It:**
- **Rule #1:** Grassroots brands PRIME visual positioning ALWAYS
- **Rule #2:** Grassroots partners get revenue share BEFORE operational costs
- **Rule #3:** Alumni speak SECOND, not first
- **Rule #4:** Public acknowledgment at every event

**The Litmus Test:**
After every interaction, grassroots partners should feel:
- "They saw us"
- "They valued us"
- "We built this together"

**If They Feel:** "They helped us" → We failed
**If They Feel:** "They amplified what we already built" → Success

---

### 2.4 The Gen Z Authenticity Gap

**Failure Scenario:**
We position as "Gen Z champions" but actual Gen Z creators build something cooler, more authentic. We look like parents trying to be relevant. Youth demographic we claim to represent ignores us.

**Why It Happens:**
- Institutional framing instead of youth-led energy
- Alumni talking ABOUT youth instead of amplifying youth voices
- "We understand Gen Z" when we don't

**How We Prevent It:**
- **Rule #1:** Youth councils have actual power, not advisory roles
- **Rule #2:** Youth OWN the spotlight, alumni stay backstage
- **Rule #3:** We're the platform, not the prophets
- **Rule #4:** Co-creation, not consultation

**The Authenticity Test:**
Who makes the decisions about youth programming?
- **If alumni/board:** Failed
- **If youth council:** Success

4. **The Convergence Declaration:** Co-created statement read by grassroots + alumni together

**The Emotion Designed:** Respect. Partnership. Not charity.

---

**Activity Zones (Designed for Circuit Completion):**

**Zone 1: Boxing Tournament (Talent Showcase)**
- Grassroots: Youth vs youth matches (pro tournament format)
- Alumni: Ringside mentoring (between rounds, not during)
- Circuit completion moment: Alumni boxers offer to train grassroots fighters
- **Invisible design:** No politicians can claim credit for 4 AM training

- Grassroots: Try ChatGPT with alumni guidance
- Alumni: Demo volunteers, not lecturers

- Rule: Alumni must ask 3 questions about grassroots participant's story before sharing own
- Circuit completion moment: Exchange phone numbers (that's it - no promises)

1. **Grassroots leaders speak FIRST** about what they'll build next

**The Emotion Designed:** Partnership. Anticipation. Not one-off event.
