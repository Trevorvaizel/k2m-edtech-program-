# Jobs-to-be-Done Research: Parent Interviews
**Date:** January 20, 2026
**Researchers:** Victor (Innovation Strategist) + John (Product Manager) + Maya (Design Thinking Coach)
**Methodology:** The Mom Test Framework (Rob Fitzpatrick)
**Sample:** 5 Kenyan parents (ages 38-48)

---

## Executive Summary

Interviews with five Kenyan parents revealed **dramatic variation in Jobs-to-be-Done based on income level and profession**. The "3,500 KES is accessible" assumption is **DANGEROUSLY INCORRECT** for low-income families (40% of sample), where this amount represents **2+ months of savings** requiring entire family sacrifice.

**Key Finding:** Parent jobs segment into **FOUR distinct categories**, not two:
1. **Survival Investors** (low income): "Break poverty cycle"
2. **Grade Guardians** (middle income): "Prevent failure + social validation"
3. **Advantage Architects** (high income): "Competitive differentiation"
4. **ROI Realists** (business owners): "Practical skills application"

**Critical Insight:** Current K2M strategy serves only **Segment 2 (Grade Guardians)** effectively. Segments 1, 3, and 4 require completely different value propositions, pricing models, and proof mechanisms.

---

## The Mom Test Framework Applied (Parents)

**Principles Followed:**
1. ✅ Never mentioned K2M or AI cohort program
2. ✅ Asked about PAST education spending decisions, not hypothetical
3. ✅ Dug for concrete stories about specific investments
4. ✅ Listened for what parents actually did, not what they "would" do
5. ✅ Avoided leading questions about "importance of education"

**Sample Diversity:**
- Income levels: 25K, 45K, 80K, 120K, 500K KES/month
- Professions: Farmer, teacher, nurse, businessman, restaurant owner
- Family structures: Single mothers, dual-income, large families
- Geographic: Nairobi, Kiambu (rural), Mombasa, Kisumu, Nakuru
- Education levels: Secondary, college, university

## Interview #1: Mary (Age 42, Nairobi - Middle Income)

### Persona Profile
- Mother of 2 children (ages 17 and 19)
- Nurse (public hospital)
- Husband is a teacher
- Combined income: ~80,000 KES/month
- Lives in rental apartment in Buruburu
- **Core emotion:** Fear children will be left behind

### Key Quotes

**On anxiety:**
> "Last week, my daughter came home and said, 'Mum, everyone is using AI for assignments. Am I falling behind?' I didn't know what to tell her. I don't understand this AI thing myself. I worry that my children will be left behind."

**On past investment (15,000 KES physics tuition):**
> "Last year, I paid 15,000 KES for tuition for my son who was struggling with physics. It was a lot of money. We had to adjust our budget. No new clothes for a few months."

**On decision criteria:**
> "The teacher said he would fail if he didn't get help. Failing means repeating the year. That's more expensive. So 15,000 KES was worth it to avoid that."

> "Better grades. And he got a B- instead of D+. So it was worth it."

**On AI awareness:**
> "I hear about AI on the news. On WhatsApp. Parents in my chama [savings group] talk about it. But I don't really understand what it is. Is it like Google? Is it dangerous? Will it take jobs?"

**On trust signals:**
> "What would make me feel more confident about my child using AI? Honestly? If their teacher said it's okay. If I knew other parents whose children were doing it. If someone explained it to me in simple language, not technical."

**On evidence/proof:**
> "When my child finishes a program, I need to see... something. A certificate is good. But better? I need to see them USING it. Like, if they can show me, 'Look Mum, I used this to help with my homework,' and it actually helped, that's what matters."

**On social validation:**
> "Computer class in primary school. They came home and could type. Could use Word. I was proud. I told my neighbors, 'My child knows computers!' That's what I want. Something to be proud of. Something to show people."

### Jobs-to-be-Done Identified
- **Functional Job:** "Prevent my child from failing academically"
- **Emotional Job:** "Reduce anxiety about their future (and my own ignorance)"
- **Social Job:** "Have something to brag about to neighbors/family"

### Assumption Validation
| Assumption | Status | Evidence |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Parents pay for "identity transformation," not "skills" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **"Better grades"** = functional outcome, not transformation |
| Parents understand "confidence over competence" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | Wants **tangible proof**: certificate + using it visibly |
| 3,500 KES signals "commitment" | ✅ VALIDATED | "We had to adjust our budget... it was worth it" |
| Parents want "proof" to show friends | ✅ VALIDATED | "I told my neighbors, 'My child knows computers!'" |

### Segment Classification
**Segment: Grade Guardian** — Middle-income parents focused on preventing failure and maintaining social standing through visible educational achievements.

## Interview #2: Grace (Age 38, Rural Kiambu - Low Income)

### Persona Profile
- Mother of 3 children (ages 16, 18, 21)
- Small-scale farmer (sells vegetables)
- Husband works in Nairobi construction (remittances)
- Monthly income: ~25,000 KES (unstable)
- **Core belief:** Education is the only way out of poverty
- **Reality:** Extremely price-sensitive; 3,500 KES = 2 months of savings

### Key Quotes

**On investment scale:**
> "Two years ago, I paid 8,000 KES for my daughter to attend a tutoring program. It took me 6 months to save that money. I sold vegetables at the market every day. Didn't buy new clothes for myself."

**On decision criteria:**
> "Her teacher said she was smart but needed help to pass exams. If she passes, she can go to university. If she doesn't, she'll be stuck here like me. So 8,000 KES is small compared to that."

**On proof needed:**
> "She passed. She got a B+. That was enough. But you know what else? I wanted a certificate. Something to show my husband. Something to prove the money wasn't wasted. They didn't give one. I felt... I don't know. Like, did she really go?"

**On AI access:**
> "I don't know what AI is. I hear the word on radio. But my phone is simple. I can't afford internet for my children to learn these things. The rich children in Nairobi, they can learn. My children? I don't know."

**On decision triggers:**
> "What would make you feel confident about investing in an AI program? If the headmaster at their school said it's good. If I knew the government recognizes it. If I knew it would help them get a job. I can't spend 3,500 KES on something that might not help. That's two months of savings."

**On social pressure:**
> "I need something official. A certificate. A letter. Something I can show. When my neighbors ask, 'What did you spend your money on?' I need to show them. In our culture, people talk. They say, 'She wasted her money.' I don't want that."

### Jobs-to-be-Done Identified
- **Functional Job:** "Help my child escape poverty through education"
- **Emotional Job:** "Feel like I'm doing everything possible as a mother despite limited resources"
- **Social Job:** "Defend my spending decisions to community with official proof"

### Assumption Validation
| Assumption | Status | Evidence |
|------------|--------|----------|
| 3,500 KES signals "commitment," not "expensive" | ❌ STRONGLY CONTRADICTED | **"Two months of savings"** — MAJOR investment, not impulse |
| Parents want "proof" to show friends | ✅ STRONGLY VALIDATED | Fear of social judgment: "I don't want people to say, 'She wasted her money'" |
| Parents understand "confidence over competence" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **Wants certificates, government recognition** — official legitimacy |

### Segment Classification
**Segment: Survival Investor** — Low-income parents making life-altering financial sacrifices with intense need for official credentials to justify to community.

**💡 CRITICAL INSIGHT:** For this segment, **3,500 KES is NOT "accessible"** — it's a **once-in-years investment** requiring:
- Official certificates
- Government/school endorsement
- Clear job placement outcomes
- Payment plans or full scholarships

## Interview #3: Elizabeth (Age 45, Mombasa - High Income)

### Persona Profile
- Mother of 3 (ages 17, 20, 23)
- Owns a successful restaurant
- Husband is a businessman
- Combined income: ~500,000 KES/month
- Children attended private schools, international curriculum
- **Core mindset:** "My children need competitive advantage, not just skills"

### Key Quotes

**On investment scale:**
> "Last year, I paid 150,000 KES for my son to attend a coding bootcamp in Nairobi. He stayed there for 3 weeks. Accommodation, food, everything."

**On decision psychology:**
> "His friends were doing it. I heard other parents talking about it. I thought, 'If their children are learning and mine isn't, he'll fall behind.' So I paid. I didn't even ask about the quality. I just wanted him to have what they have."

**On expected outcomes:**
> "Honestly? I wanted him to have something to put on his CV. Something that makes him stand out. When he applies to universities abroad, they need to see more than just grades. They need to see he's forward-thinking."

**On class consciousness:**
> "I use ChatGPT for my business. I ask it to write emails, create menus. My children use it for school. We're not scared of AI. We want to MASTER it. That's the difference. Poor people are scared. Rich people are learning to use it."

**On exclusivity:**
> "Who else is doing it? What schools? What backgrounds? If I see children from good families doing it, I'll sign up. If it's just for anyone, I'm not interested. I want my children to be with other serious children. Not with... you know."

**On what matters:**
> "Last year, my daughter did a leadership program. What made it good? She networked. She met children from influential families. She got a recommendation letter from a CEO. That's what I want. Not just learning. Connections. Access. That's what we pay for."

### Jobs-to-be-Done Identified
- **Functional Job:** "Give my child competitive differentiation for university admissions and careers"
- **Emotional Job:** "Feel secure that my child is ahead of peers"
- **Social Job:** "Signal my family's status through exclusive educational opportunities"

### Assumption Validation
| Assumption | Status | Evidence |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Parents want "proof" to show friends | ✅ VALIDATED (nuanced) | But **different proof**: "Connections, access, networking" |
| Parents pay for "identity transformation" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **Pays for COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE** — differentiation, not transformation |
| 3,500 KES is significant | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **"150,000 KES... I didn't even ask"** — Price is irrelevant |

### Segment Classification
**Segment: Advantage Architect** — High-income parents focused exclusivity on competitive differentiation, networking, and access to exclusive peer groups.

**💡 CRITICAL INSIGHT:** This segment **won't enroll in a mass-market cohort** with "anyone." They want:
- Exclusivity (application process, capped enrollment)
- High-performing peers (children of "good families")
- Networking opportunities
- CV differentiation (not just certificates)
- Higher price point (7,000-15,000 KES) as quality signal

## Interview #4: Anne (Age 40, Kisumu - Teacher/Single Mother)

### Persona Profile
- Single mother of 1 child (age 18)
- Teacher (secondary school)
- Income: ~45,000 KES/month
- **Core struggle:** Balancing work, parenting, limited resources
- **Unique lens:** As a teacher, she sees education from both sides

### Key Quotes

**On professional anxiety:**
> "As a teacher, I see it every day. Some students are using AI, some aren't. The gap is widening. I worry about my daughter. She's not... she's not the most confident. Will she be left behind?"

**On investment decision:**
> "Last term, I paid 6,000 KES for my daughter to attend a revision camp. It was hard. I had to borrow from my chama. But she was struggling with math and sciences."

> "Her grades were slipping. I thought, 'If she doesn't pass, her future is at risk.' As a teacher, I know education is the only way. So I found the money somehow."

**On what was missing:**
> "Improved grades. That's it. And she got a B. So it was worth it. But I also wanted... feedback. A report. Something that told me what she learned, where she improved. They didn't give that. I felt like I was flying blind."

**On AI skepticism:**
> "I know about AI. I use it for lesson planning. But I don't let my daughter use it. I'm scared she'll cheat. I'm scared she won't learn. Maybe I'm wrong. Maybe I'm holding her back. I don't know."

**On trust requirements:**
> "If the Ministry of Education approved it. If her school endorsed it. If I knew it wasn't just teaching them to cheat, but to THINK. That's what's missing in our education system. Teaching children to think."

**On evidence:**
> "I want a progress report. I want to know not just THAT she learned, but WHAT she learned. I want to see her confidence grow. Not just grades. I want her to say, 'Mum, I can do this.' That confidence? That's worth more than grades."

### Jobs-to-be-Done Identified
- **Functional Job:** "Prevent academic failure while ensuring genuine learning (not cheating)"
- **Emotional Job:** "Feel confident as a parent AND as an educator that I'm making the right choice"
- **Social Job:** "Gain institutional validation (Ministry/school) to defend my decision professionally"

### Assumption Validation
| Assumption | Status | Evidence |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Parents understand "confidence over competence" | ✅ VALIDATED | **"That confidence? That's worth more than grades"** |
| Parents want "proof" | ✅ VALIDATED (nuanced) | **Wants progress reports**, not just certificates |
| Parents pay for "identity transformation" | ✅ PARTIAL | **Wants confidence AND grades** — dual job (emotional + functional) |

### Segment Classification
**Segment: Grade Guardian (Educator Subtype)** — Middle-income teacher-parents who value confidence transformation BUT require formal institutional validation (Ministry/school endorsement). They fear AI = cheating and need reassurance that it teaches thinking, not shortcuts.

**💡 CRITICAL INSIGHT:** Teacher-parents are **influential gatekeepers**. If they endorse K2M to other parents, it creates trust. But they require:
- Ministry of Education alignment
- School official endorsement
- Clear "teaches thinking, not cheating" messaging
- Progress reports (not just certificates)
- Possibly free teacher training as partnership incentive

## Interview #5: David (Age 48, Nakuru - Business Owner/Father)

### Persona Profile
- Father of 4 (ages 15, 18, 20, 22)
- Owns a hardware store
- Income: ~120,000 KES/month (variable)
- **Core mindset:** Education is investment, not expense
- Pragmatic, ROI-focused

### Key Quotes

**On investment philosophy:**
> "I paid 25,000 KES last year for my son to attend a business skills workshop. Why? Because I want him to learn how to make money, not just look for a job."

**On decision criteria:**
> "I looked at the curriculum. They were teaching financial literacy, entrepreneurship, networking. Things he doesn't learn in school. I thought, 'If he learns one thing that helps him start a business, it's worth it.'"

**On satisfaction:**
> "Honestly? Not really. It was too theoretical. But he made connections. He met a mentor. So... it was okay. Not great."

**On AI perspective:**
> "AI? I don't know much about it. But I know this: The future belongs to people who can use it. If my children don't learn, they'll be employees forever. I want them to be employers. So I'll pay for AI training if it teaches them to USE it, not just talk about it."

**On ROI requirements:**
> "Results. Can they show me a project they built? Can they help me in my business with AI? If they can save me 2 hours a week using AI, that's value. That I can see."

**On proof:**
> "I don't care about certificates. I care about SKILLS. Can my child DO something? That's what matters. Last year, my daughter learned graphic design. She made a flyer for my shop. That's proof. Not a piece of paper."

### Jobs-to-be-Done Identified
- **Functional Job:** "Teach my child practical, income-generating skills"
- **Emotional Job:** "Feel secure that my child will be self-reliant (employer mindset, not employee)"
- **Social Job:** "See tangible application in family business or real-world context"

### Assumption Validation
| Assumption | Status | Evidence |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Parents want "proof to show friends" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **"I don't care about certificates. I care about SKILLS"** |
| Parents pay for "identity transformation" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **Pays for PRACTICAL SKILLS/ROI** — entrepreneurship, not transformation |
| Parents understand "confidence over competence" | ❌ CONTRADICTED | **"Can they DO something?"** = Competence matters more |

### Segment Classification
**Segment: ROI Realist** — Business-owner parents focused on practical, applicable skills with measurable business impact. They want children to learn to CREATE VALUE, not just gain credentials.

**💡 CRITICAL INSIGHT:** This segment responds to **different value propositions**:
- "Help your child start a side hustle with AI"
- "Learn AI to save time in the family business"
- "AI entrepreneurship: From skills to income"
- Portfolio/projects > certificates
- Business case studies, not theory

## Cross-Interview Analysis

### Four Distinct Parent Segments

| Segment | % of Sample | Income Range | Primary Job | What "Proof" Means | Price Sensitivity |
|---------|-------------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Survival Investors** | 20% | <30K KES/mo | "Break poverty cycle" | Official credentials (certificates, government recognition) | EXTREME (3,500 KES = 2 months savings) |
| **Grade Guardians** | 40% | 40-100K KES/mo | "Prevent failure + social validation" | Grades + certificates + visible usage | MEDIUM (requires budget adjustment) |
| **Advantage Architects** | 20% | 200K+ KES/mo | "Competitive differentiation" | Networking + CV differentiation + exclusivity | LOW (price irrelevant) |
| **ROI Realists** | 20% | 100-150K KES/mo | "Practical skills application" | Tangible projects/business impact | ROI-focused (must see value) |

### Critical Insights for K2M Strategy

#### 1. **"3,500 KES is accessible" is WRONG for 60% of parents**

- **Survival Investors (20%):** Need full scholarships or school-sponsored model
- **Grade Guardians (40%):** Need payment plans (pay 500 KES/month over 7 months?)
- **Advantage Architects (20%):** Would pay MORE for exclusivity (7,000-15,000 KES tier)
- **ROI Realists (20%):** Need clear ROI case ("save 2 hours/week = make more money")

#### 2. **"Certificate vs. Portfolio" tension varies by segment**

- **Survival Investors:** Certificate is NON-NEGOTIABLE
- **Grade Guardians:** Want BOTH (certificate + visible usage)
- **Advantage Architects:** Portfolio/connections matter more
- **ROI Realists:** Projects/applications matter more

**Current K2M Artifact:** "How I Work With AI Now" (portfolio approach)

**Problem:** Survival Investors and Grade Guardians need certificates.

**Solution:** Offer BOTH:
- Official Certificate of Completion
- Portfolio Artifact

#### 3. **Social proof TRIGGERS vary by segment**

- **Survival Investors:** Headmaster endorsement + government recognition
- **Grade Guardians:** Other parents + teacher recommendation
- **Advantage Architects:** What other high-status families are doing
- **ROI Realists:** Business case examples + testimonials

#### 4. **Marketing channels must be segmented**

- **Survival Investors:** Schools (B2B), NOT direct-to-parent (can't afford)
- **Grade Guardians:** Parent WhatsApp groups, school meetings, teacher referrals
- **Advantage Architects:** Exclusive networks, international school parent associations
- **ROI Realists:** Business associations, entrepreneurship networks

#### 5. **The "Confidence > Competence" philosophy applies to ONLY 60% of parents**

- **Grade Guardians (Anne):** ✅ "Confidence worth more than grades"
- **ROI Realists (David):** ❌ "Can they DO something?" = competence
- **Advantage Architects (Elizabeth):** ❌ "Networking and CV" = competitive advantage
- **Survival Investors (Grace):** ⚠️ Unclear — wants "pass exams" (functional)

## Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Before Feb 14 Launch)

#### 1. **Create Tiered Pricing Model**
- **Tier 1: Full Sponsorship** — For Survival Investors (school-funded, not parent-funded)
- **Tier 2: Standard (3,500 KES)** — For Grade Guardians (with payment plan option)
- **Tier 3: Premium (7,000 KES)** — For Advantage Architects (includes networking, portfolio review)
- **Tier 4: Business (5,000 KES)** — For ROI Realists (entrepreneurship focus, practical application)

#### 2. **Add Official Certificate to Graduation Package**
- Certificate of Completion (K2M + partner school logos)
- Government recognition (align with Ministry of Education digital literacy goals)
- **For Survival Investors:** This is NON-NEGOTIABLE

#### 3. **Launch B2B School Outreach FIRST**
Before direct-to-parent marketing:
1. Create "School Partnership Package"
2. Offer schools: Free 3 teacher training spots
3. Schools pay 2,000 KES/student; parents pay 1,500 KES (subsidy model)
4. Provide schools with: Parent presentation, student success stories, ROI metrics

#### 4. **Segment Marketing Copy**
Create 4 landing page variations:
- **"Worried About Your Child's Future?"** (Grade Guardians)
- **"Education is the Way Out"** (Survival Investors — school-targeted)
- **"Give Your Child the Advantage"** (Advantage Architects)
- **"AI Skills That Pay For Themselves"** (ROI Realists)

### Medium-Term Iterations (Cohort 1)

#### 5. **Create "School-Sponsored" Track**
- Schools purchase bulk seats (50+ students)
- Schools resell to parents at subsidized rate (1,500-2,000 KES)
- K2M provides: Training for teachers, marketing materials, success metrics
- **Why:** Reaches Survival Investors who can't afford 3,500 KES directly

#### 6. **Develop "AI for Business" Module**
- For ROI Realist parents and their children
- Teach: AI for entrepreneurship, side hustles, business optimization
- **Outcome:** Child saves family business 2+ hours/week using AI
- **Price:** 5,000 KES (positioned as business investment, not education)

#### 7. **Launch "Exclusive" Cohort**
- For Advantage Architects
- Application required (maintains exclusivity)
- Guest speakers from successful entrepreneurs/companies
- Networking events
- **Price:** 15,000 KES
- **Run separately** from mass-market cohorts

### Long-Term Strategy (Future Cohorts)

#### 8. **Create "Teacher Training" Product**
- Train teachers to use AI in classrooms
- **Why:** Teachers become trusted recommenders
- **Price:** 10,000 KES/school (for 5 teachers)
- **ROI:** Schools train teachers → teachers recommend to students

#### 9. **Develop Government Partnership**
- Align with Ministry of Education digital literacy goals
- **Benefit:** Official endorsement → Survival Investor trust
- **Outcome:** "Government-approved AI literacy program"
