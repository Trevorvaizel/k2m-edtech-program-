## BEFORE YOU WALK IN
*(What you carry into the conversation)*

The pastor is not a customer.
They are a shepherd.
Their entire identity is built around guiding their flock
through the wilderness — and right now, their Form 4
leavers are standing at the entrance to one.

You are not selling a program.
You are offering their youth a road through the gap.

Arrive as someone who has been thinking about their congregation.
Not someone who has been thinking about sales targets.

---

## WHEN TO APPROACH

**Best moments:**
- After Sunday service, briefly, before they are surrounded
- By appointment — "I have something for your Form 4 youth, 10 minutes"
- Through a church elder or deacon who can introduce you first
- Via WhatsApp request before appearing in person

**Never:**
- Mid-service
- When they are counselling someone
- Cold, with no introduction, at the gate

---

## THE WALK-IN SCRIPT

### THE OPENER
*(30 seconds. Warm. Respectful of their role.)*

> "Good morning Pastor [NAME]. My name is Trevor. I run a program called K2M and I've been thinking about your Form 4 youth — the ones who just finished their exams and are now in that gap before university."

*Brief pause. Let them connect to the image.*

> "I know that window can go either way for young people. We're running a pioneering program in partnership with Le Wagon — a French institution with campuses in 44 cities globally — and we're selecting 5 congregations in Nairobi whose youth leaders believe their young people deserve more than just waiting. I'd love 10 minutes of your time."

---

### ACT 1 — IN THE ROOM
*(Once seated — 2 minutes)*

> "Pastor, you know better than anyone what the gap year looks like for young people who don't have structure. They finished Form 4 and now they're home. Waiting. And the waiting is where things go wrong."

> "K2M is an 8-week cohort for Form 4 leavers. We give them 4 structured thinking habits — and by the end of 8 weeks, each student has navigated a subject they've never formally studied and produced something real to show for it. Not a promise. Actual work."

> "We're running this in association with Le Wagon France — their graduates have gone on to careers at Google, HSBC, Airbnb, and leading firms across East Africa. In Kenya, this is the first time a program of this kind is being offered at the pre-university level, through a church partnership."

*Then the pivot:*

> "We are not selling this to your youth. K2M is sponsoring 75% of the program cost for every pioneer student your congregation puts forward. Your role is simply to identify them."

---

### ACT 2 — THE PIONEER OFFER
*(60 seconds — the heart of the conversation)*

> "Here is how the partnership works:"

> "We select 5 congregations. You nominate 20 of your Form 4 leavers — the young people you know are ready to take their future seriously. K2M covers 75% of the program cost for each of them. Their contribution is [PIONEER_FEE] KES — against a program that is worth [FULL_FEE] KES."

> "Why are we doing this? Because we need a pioneer cohort — real young people, real transformation — to show what this program does in the Kenyan context. Your congregation gets to say your youth were among the founding generation of this. Those students carry a Pioneer designation that travels with them to campus and beyond."

*Then — enter their spiritual territory:*

> "Pastor, the gap year is not a waiting room. It is a formation moment. What a young person becomes in those 8 months before university determines a great deal of who they are when they get there. We want to be in that moment with your youth — not instead of the church, but alongside it."

---

### ACT 3 — THE TESTIMONY MOMENT
*(2 minutes — this replaces the CBC demo used for teachers)*

> "Let me tell you what this looks like in practice."

> "A Form 4 leaver — let's call her Amina. She finishes her exams in November. By December she's home, uncertain, scrolling. No structure. No direction. She knows university is coming but it feels abstract."

> "In Week 1 of K2M, we ask her to pick a domain she's never studied — economics, law, medicine, policy, business. Something that intimidates her. By Week 8, she has navigated that domain using structured thinking and produced an artifact — a real piece of work that shows she understood it."

> "She arrives at campus having already done something hard. She arrives knowing she can figure things out she's never been taught. That is a different kind of young person than the one who spent 8 months waiting."

*Then look at the pastor directly:*

> "That is the young person you've been praying your congregation's youth would become. We are offering to help build that — for 20 of them, starting now."

---

### ACT 4 — THE CLOSE
*(One ask. One door.)*

> "I need one thing from you: a list of 20. The Form 4 leavers in your congregation you would stake your name on. Young people who are ready."

> "We have two orientation dates: [DATE + TIME] and [DATE + TIME]. Which one works for your youth to come together?"

*Stop. Let them answer.*

---

### HANDLING THE KEY MOMENTS

**If they ask: "What is Le Wagon exactly?"**
> "Le Wagon is a French coding bootcamp — 44 campuses globally, including London, Paris, Singapore. They partner with top universities worldwide. We're running this cohort in association with them as the foundational thinking layer — K2M teaches your youth to think. Le Wagon is the technical pathway that follows later. But what your youth get from K2M is immediately useful, at any university, in any subject."

**If they ask: "Is this a Christian program?"**
> "K2M is not a religious program — it is a thinking program. But the values align completely: we believe every young person has potential that deserves to be developed. We believe the gap year is a formation moment, not a wasted one. We believe access to world-class education should not depend on family connections or money. That is why we are sponsoring 75% of the cost for your youth."

**If they ask: "75% — who covers the rest?"**
> "K2M covers 75%. The student's contribution is [PIONEER_FEE] KES. That small amount matters — because when a young person has invested something, they show up differently. The program works better when students have skin in the game. But the full [FULL_FEE] KES value? K2M carries that weight for every pioneer student."

**If they ask: "What do I tell the parents?"**
> "Tell them their child was identified by their pastor as someone worth investing in. Tell them K2M is investing [75% of FULL_FEE] KES in their child. Tell them the program runs for 8 weeks online, it is structured and supervised, and by the end their child will have proof of something they built with their own thinking. I can send you a parent brief you can share directly."

**If they say: "Let me pray about it":**
> "Of course, Pastor. I respect that deeply. We are finalising the 5 congregations this week — I'll hold your slot until [DATE]. If it feels right after that, we move together. If not, no pressure at all."

**If they ask: "How do I know this is legitimate?"**
> "Le Wagon France has been running for over 10 years with campuses in 44 cities. I'm happy to share their profile with you. I'm also happy for you to speak with any of the teachers or principals who have already seen the program. What I'd love more than anything is for you to see a live demo — let me show you what a student experiences in Week 1. Five minutes."
