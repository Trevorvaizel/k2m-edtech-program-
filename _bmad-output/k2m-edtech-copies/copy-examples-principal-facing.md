# Copy Application: Principal-Facing Cohort Promotion

**Testing the Attention Framework for School Leaders**

---

## Validated Principal JTBD

**Core Job:** "Have something to tell parents at admissions meetings that makes us look modern and keeps enrollment stable"

**Validated Anxieties:**
- "Keeping up with other schools" – competitive pressure
- "Marketing tool" – need differentiation to attract students
- "Burned by vendors" – wary of over-promising EdTech
- "Easy to implement" – no infrastructure, no training burden
- "Proof from schools like mine" – need validation from similar contexts

**Validated Language:**
- "Differentiators," "competitive advantage"
- "Modern," "innovative"
- "Turnkey solution"
- "Implementation challenges," "infrastructure constraints"

---

## Current Copy Analysis

### What Works
✅ Story-based opening (creates emotional connection)
✅ Vivid problem description ("pretending to understand," "YouTube at 2am")
✅ Clear solution positioning
✅ Community aspect ("200 students")

### What Could Be Stronger
❌ **Abstraction leakage:** "Structured learning," "AI conversations" – too vague
❌ **Missing M2 (Expose):** Doesn't name the principal's invisible script about competitive pressure
❌ **Missing M3 (Pressure):** Doesn't show cost of NOT having this program
❌ **Generic CTA:** "Join 200 students" doesn't create urgency or differentiation
❌ **Price justification:** Focuses on "cheap" instead of "investment in competitiveness"

---

**Why this works:**

**M2 (Expose):** Names the invisible script – principals struggle with parent questions about AI
**M1 (Destabilize):** Story creates visceral tension – "everyone else knows things they don't"
**M3 (Pressure):** Shows competitive cost – "some schools have good answers, others hope parents don't ask"
**M5 (Identity):** "Some schools..." creates aspiration – very few principals want to be in the "hoping" category
**M6 (Agency):** Returns choice – "you're choosing which story to tell"
**M7 (Exit):** Open question – "what does it cost to say your school prepares students?"

**Test:**
- 3-second comprehension? Yes (story is immediate)
- Validates anxiety? Yes (parent questions, competitive pressure)
- Creates urgency? Yes (university timeline + competitive differentiation)
- Abstraction leakage? None (all concrete outcomes)
- Returns agency? Yes ("you're choosing which story")

### Framework-Applied Improvements

## Summary: What Works for Principals

1. **Name their skepticism first** – "Most principals have been burned by EdTech vendors"
2. **Show competitive pressure** – "Other schools are already answering parents' AI questions"
3. **Remove implementation burden** – "Zero infrastructure. No teacher training."
4. **Give admissions language** – "Prepare students for AI" = talking point
5. **Make it about differentiation** – "Some schools can say yes with confidence"
6. **Return agency without pressure** – "You're choosing which story to tell"

---

## The Framework Works When...

✅ First sentence validates their pain (skepticism, competitive pressure)
✅ Shows cost of inaction (losing students to more "modern" schools)
✅ Removes implementation barriers (zero burden on school)
✅ Gives concrete language for admissions ("prepare students for AI")
✅ Creates aspiration (very few principals want to be in "hoping" category)

## The Framework Fails When...

❌ First sentence announces features
❌ Focuses on student outcomes without principal benefits
❌ Creates implementation burden ("requires teacher training")
❌ Generic competitive claims ("best AI program")
❌ CTA doesn't address differentiation
