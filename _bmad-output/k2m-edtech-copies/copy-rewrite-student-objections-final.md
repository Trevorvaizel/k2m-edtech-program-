## Why This Works

### The Framework Applied

**M1 (Destabilize) – "Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it"**
- Validates truth without being defensive
- Creates immediate curiosity gap
- Invokes: "what's the difference?"

**M2 (Expose) – "Some students copy-paste answers. They find a prompt..."**
- Names the invisible script without judgment
- Makes it specific and relatable
- Creates recognition: "that's what I do"

**M1 (Story) – "Then they face a real problem... ChatGPT gives generic response..."**
- Shows the problem, don't explain it
- Builds tension through concrete scenario
- Makes reader feel the stuck

**M4 (Reframe) – "They understand what they're actually asking for"**
- Dissolves old question ("which prompts?")
- Introduces higher-order question ("understanding what you're asking for")
- Makes the shift feel inevitable

**M3 (Pressure) – "The habits you form now will follow you into university"**
- Shows inevitable cost, not threat
- "Training yourself to depend" = compounding effect
- Makes staying costly

**M5 (Identity) – "Some students will arrive... Others will figure it out"**
- Creates aspiration without saying "you should"
- "Some students" = very few, creates differentiation desire
- Makes it about who you want to be

**M6 (Agency) – "Do you want to arrive ready, or figure it out when you're already behind?"**
- Returns choice without rescue
- No "you should do this" – just "here's what's possible"
- Makes the consequences clear

**M7 (Exit) – "What would it look like to walk into university already knowing how to think with AI?"**
- No closure, question stays active
- Invites visualization, creates after-effect
- Ends with possibility, not demand

## The After-Effect Test

**Original closing:** "The only question is whether you'll do something about it."
- Reader feels: Guilt, pressure, resistance
- Response: "Maybe later" or dismiss entirely

**Framework closing:** "What would it look like to walk into university already knowing how to think with AI?"
- Reader feels: Curiosity, visualization, aspiration
- Response: Keeps thinking about it after reading

✅ **Framework version creates "I can't unsee what you pointed at" effect**
